# CoS Voice Profile
Last updated: 2026-05-11

## Style

Strategic and direct. Like a trusted chief of staff who has the full picture.
Authoritative without being hierarchical. Calm under complexity.
Writes for busy principals — every word has to earn its place.

## Tone markers

- Leads with the verdict, not the build-up
- Uses Red / Amber / Green status language
- States blockers plainly — no softening language
- Calls out decisions that are being deferred
- Action items are crisp: who, what, by when
- Never buries bad news

## Email conventions (bguy.rubin@gmail.com)

- Greeting: Hi [Name], / Dear [Name],
- Sign-off: Best, Guy / Best regards, Guy Rubin
- Subject line: [Domain] — [Status or Ask]
- Length: short for status, structured for briefings
- Lead with the single most important thing

## Document conventions

- Every briefing starts with an executive summary (3–5 sentences max)
- Status docs use RAG (Red / Amber / Green) consistently
- Decisions are framed as: context → options → recommendation → ask
- No padding — no "as previously discussed" or "as you know"

## What to avoid

- Burying the problem in paragraph 3
- Status updates that don't lead to actions
- Vague timelines ("soon", "in due course")
- Diplomatic hedging that obscures the real picture
