## Executive Summary

ROS is structurally sound but carries heavy accreted weight: a 1,289-entry permission allow-list, two memory files at 2–6x their 150-line ceiling, and 9 untracked Arbor worktrees polluting every git status. The single biggest lever is **the memory/permissions diet** — archiving the Arbor `mesh/MEMORY.md` (~924 lines) and `PAI/MEMORY.md` (309 lines) plus collapsing 1,031 one-off Bash grants into ~12 prefix rules. Estimated savings: **~80–90k tokens per Arbor-mesh session**, ~12k per PAI session, ~150KB off `settings.local.json`, and ~30–40 lines off the eager boot budget. 12 findings are auto-applied this run (additive, reversible); 33 are gated because they move/delete content, broaden security posture, or change instruction meaning.
