Step 2

Safety triage

MVP rule: classify conservatively. High and urgent cases require human review.

Classification rationale

This is a repeated transition/routine problem with mild aggression toward objects. No urgent medical or safety red flags are present in the intake. Because it has repeated for three weeks and affects daycare transitions, classify as Medium: structured plan + 7-day tracking + escalation if worsening or cross-setting impairment appears.

Step 3

Guidance response

Read

This looks like a repeated transition problem, not a character problem. The pattern matters more than one difficult morning. Your child may be struggling with switching from safe/home mode into separation/daycare mode.

Risk Level

Medium — not urgent based on current information, but persistent enough to track and structure.

Do Today

  • Prepare one visual 3-step morning sequence: bathroom → clothes → shoes.
  • Offer only two choices: “blue shoes or black shoes?” not “what do you want to do?”
  • Name the transition calmly: “Now we move from home mode to daycare mode.”
  • Do not negotiate during the meltdown; return to the same short phrase.
  • After daycare, praise recovery specifically: “You were upset and still got through the door.”

Track for 7 days

  • Start time of meltdown
  • Trigger
  • Duration
  • What helped recovery
  • Whether daycare drop-off itself was okay after arrival

Escalate if

Escalate to a professional if aggression increases, distress appears across multiple settings, sleep/appetite changes significantly, separation distress worsens, or daycare reports persistent impairment.
