## 2. The principle: the child is the angle in, the PARENT is the customer

The Hero Comic is the **lure** — it stops a busy, worried parent mid-scroll and earns the registration. But **the parent is who pays, returns, and refers.** A registration that gets only a cute comic churns. A registration where the **parent** also gets a real win *for themselves* in the first session converts to a subscription. So:

> **Acquire through the child. Convert and retain through parent value.**
> Lure: *"make your kid a superhero."* Payoff for the parent, same session: *"…and finally understand what's going on with them, and what to do tonight."*

### Stage 1 — the angle in (acquire the registration)
*"Create your child's superhero comic — free."* → land → **register (Apple/Google one-tap, no card)** → make the HeroAvatar → get a shareable comic. It's a gift about their child, instant, only real after they register, and they *want* to post it (the K engine). Second door for the worried segment: the **2am hook** ("stop fear-Googling your kid"). Both lead to the same registration.

### Stage 2 — the parent's win (what turns it into a subscription)
The moment they're in, hand the **parent** a real result, not just the kid's toy:
- ask one real worry ("she won't sleep") → an immediate **calm Read / Risk-level / Do-tonight** answer that uses *their* child's context → the **"this app actually gets my kid"** aha.
- a peek at **Rhythm** ("tomorrow's likely friction window: ~5pm") and today's **Daily Play** chosen for *their* kid.
