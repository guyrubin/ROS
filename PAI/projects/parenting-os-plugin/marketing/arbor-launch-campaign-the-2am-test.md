## 1. The Big Idea

Every parent has a **2am moment**: kid won't sleep, said something scary, spiked a fever, bit another kid — and you're alone in the dark, thumb-typing panic into Google, getting a wall of worst-case forum threads that make it *worse*.

**Arbor is what you reach for instead of Google at 2am.** Calm read, plan for tonight, and it remembers — so tomorrow's answer is even better.

The campaign weaponizes a universal, emotional, *instantly shareable* truth. We don't sell an app; we name an enemy (**fear-Googling**) and hand parents the antidote. That's how calm becomes aggressive.

> **Enemy:** the alarmist parenting internet + Dr. Google.
> **Hero:** the parent, made calm and competent.
> **Weapon:** Arbor.
