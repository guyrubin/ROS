## 60-Second Demo Script

Arbor is a developmental operating system for families. The first wedge is behavior, routines, and emotional regulation for children aged roughly 2–8.

A parent starts with a real question, like: “My 4-year-old melts down every morning before daycare.” Arbor captures only the essential context: age, situation, duration, frequency, what the parent tried, safety concerns, and the parent’s goal.

Then Arbor classifies risk conservatively. In this example it is medium: not urgent, but persistent enough to track.

Arbor gives a structured response: what is likely happening, what to do today, what to track for seven days, and when to escalate.

The important difference is memory. Arbor saves the useful pattern into a child timeline, so the next answer is not generic.

If the issue persists or needs professional input, Arbor turns the parent’s messy story into a clear handoff note for a pediatrician, educator, psychologist, speech therapist, parent coach, or family-support organization.

The core loop is: ask, understand, act, track, learn, and escalate when needed.
