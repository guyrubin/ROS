# Parent Tester Recruitment Message — Arbor

Status: Draft v1
Use: WhatsApp / email / DM
Audience: Friendly parents of children aged 2–8

---

## Short DM Version

Hi — I’m testing an early version of **Arbor**, an AI parenting support tool for everyday child-development questions.

The first version helps with things like morning routines, bedtime battles, tantrums, emotional regulation, screen-time transitions, and “is this normal or should I ask someone?” moments.

It is **not** a doctor, therapist, or diagnosis tool. The goal is simple: help parents get a calm read, a practical plan for today, what to track, and when to involve a professional.

Would you be open to trying it with 1–2 real parenting questions and giving honest feedback?

Time needed: about 15 minutes.

## Longer Email Version

Subject: Testing Arbor — early AI parenting support tool

Hi [Name],

I’m building an early version of **Arbor**, a parent-support platform for child-development questions.

The idea is not to replace doctors, therapists, teachers, or parenting experts. Arbor is meant to act like a calm co-support layer: it helps a parent describe what is happening, understand whether it looks urgent or a pattern to track, get a practical “do today” plan, and prepare a better note for a professional if needed.

For the first test, I’m focusing on parents of children aged roughly 2–8 and everyday but stressful situations like:

- morning or bedtime routines
- tantrums and emotional regulation
- screen-time transitions
- separation or daycare/school difficulty
- sibling conflict
- early “should I worry?” developmental questions

Would you be willing to test it with one or two real questions from your own family life?

What I’d ask:

1. Share a parenting situation you would genuinely want help with.
2. Read Arbor’s response.
3. Tell me whether it felt useful, safe, clear, and practical.
4. Tell me what felt wrong, generic, or missing.

Time needed: 15–20 minutes.

Important: Arbor does not diagnose, prescribe, or handle emergencies. If something is medical, urgent, or safety-related, the product should direct parents to the right professional support.

If you’re open to it, I’ll send the first intake questions.

Thanks,
Guy

---

## Screening Criteria

Good first testers:

- Parent/caregiver of child aged 2–8
- Has at least one recurring routine/behavior/emotional regulation question
- Comfortable giving direct feedback
- Understands this is not medical/therapy advice

Avoid first testers where the main issue is immediately high-risk, legal, custody-related, severe medical, abuse/neglect, or acute mental health crisis.
