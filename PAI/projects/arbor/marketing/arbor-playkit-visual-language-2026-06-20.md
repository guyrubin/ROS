## Core Direction

Arbor marketing should look like the Arbor product world, not like generic premium health-tech photography.

The correct language is:

- Hebrew-first / RTL app energy.
- Soft mint, aqua, cream, navy, coral, lavender.
- Big rounded mobile UI cards with soft shadows.
- Illustrated child hero as the emotional anchor.
- Mission/story cards, badges, progress bars, avatar chips, mascot stickers.
- Comic/adventure warmth, but still parent-trusted and polished.
- Clean empty text zones where real Hebrew/English copy is overlaid deterministically.

## Visual Rules

Use:

- Rounded white panels with large radius and airy spacing.
- Mint/aqua glow backgrounds.
- Navy primary strokes and text zones.
- Teal primary buttons and progress bars.
- Coral/orange and lavender cards for variety.
- Child hero art with expressive eyes and warm confidence.
- Adventure cards: speech, feelings, story, academy, missions.
- Small badges, shields, hearts, stars, plants, and friendly sticker elements.
- Clear hierarchy: top tabs, big hero card, three lower cards, bottom nav, CTA pill.

Avoid:

- Photorealistic parent bedroom scenes as the main campaign look.
- Dark clinical or anxiety-heavy visuals except as a tiny opening beat.
- Generic chatbot/AI-glow/brain imagery.
- Hospital/doctor imagery.
- Stock-family photography.
- Cutesy rainbow clutter.
- Text generated inside AI images, especially Hebrew.
- Any diagnostic, cure, emergency, or clinical claim.
- Copyrighted superhero marks or recognizable external characters.

## Typography And Text

Generated images should usually contain **no readable text**.

Final Hebrew and English copy must be added as a clean layer in Canva, CapCut, the app, or deterministic HTML/CSS. This avoids garbled AI Hebrew and keeps the brand credible.

Preferred overlay behavior:

- Large Hebrew headline in navy.
- Short subtitle in navy or slate.
- Teal CTA pill.
- Small Arbor logo/mark at top or end card.
- Keep line length short; this is mobile-first.

## Campaign Fit

The 2am Test still works, but visually it should be translated into the product world:

- Not: dark parent doom-scroll photo as the whole campaign.
- Yes: "the fear moment becomes an Arbor mission/card/world."
- The emotional beat is calm competence, not panic.
- The child hero and cards make the share mechanic visible.
