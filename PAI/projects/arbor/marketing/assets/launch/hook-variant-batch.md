# Hook Variant Batch — Avatar Challenge + 2am Pieces

**Asset ID:** LAUNCH-003
**Format:** First-3-seconds hook lines for 9:16 Reels / TikTok IL
**Source concepts:** The #הגיבורשלי avatar challenge (Video 2 / Section 2B from `arbor-israel-viral-content-calendar-30d.md`) and the 2am fear-Google piece (Video 1 / Section 2A)
**Hook bank destination:** `mesh/marketing/hook-bank.md`
**Production method:** These hooks are the visual + audio first 3 seconds only — not the full video. Each hook is self-contained enough to A/B test before assembling the full video. Distribution runs whichever hook wins the highest intro-retention in the first 24h.

**Hook type taxonomy (per VIRAL-ENGINE.md and brief):**
- RS = Recognized Scenario (parent sees themselves immediately)
- CC = Contrarian Correction (calls out what everyone else does wrong)
- AS = Authority Stat (credibility-first, but mechanism not effect-size)
- CG = Curiosity Gap (withholds enough to force the next second)

## BLOCK A — Avatar Challenge Hooks (#הגיבורשלי)

The visual for all Block A hooks: the first frame is the Arbor HeroAvatar of a child (the "נועה" fictional character, illustrated, not a real face) on screen — full bleed, no text yet. The hook line arrives at or just before second 2 as either on-screen text or VO. The goal: the parent stops scrolling before they consciously decide to stop.

---

### A-1 — "מה שקרה כשהראיתי לה"
**Type:** RS (Recognized Scenario) — the parent who already knows they will share this, before they have done it.

**Visual (0:00–0:03):** The HeroAvatar fills the screen. A parent's hands are visible at the bottom edge, holding the phone, turning it slightly as if about to show someone.

**On-screen text (appears at 0:01, Frank Ruhl Libre, white):**
```
מה שקרה כשהראיתי לה את עצמה.
```

**EN reference (not for screen — for brief context only):**
"What happened when I showed her herself."

**Why this works:** The recognized scenario is the parent anticipating the reveal moment. The hook withholds the reaction — the viewer watches to see it. The word "עצמה" (herself) is the trigger: the parent does not show the child a cartoon, they show the child *herself* as a hero. The distinction is real and load-bearing.

**Arbor Bar (hook-level check):**
- Memory test: "את עצמה" implies an artifact derived from the specific child. Pass.
- Enemy test: implicit (the generic cartoon vs. the personalized hero). Pass at hook level — the video carries the explicit enemy frame.
- Composure test: calm curiosity, no urgency. Pass.
- First-line test: "מה שקרה כשהראיתי לה את עצמה" — cannot run on Kinedu or Khan Kids because neither generates a personalized hero from the child's record. Pass.

**Predicted performance driver:** The combination of the visual (the hero avatar on screen) and the copy ("את עצמה") creates an immediate question: how is this the child? The answer requires watching. Highest curiosity-gap score in this batch.

---

### A-2 — "90 שניות"
**Type:** CG (Curiosity Gap) + RS — the disbelief at the speed.

**Visual (0:00–0:03):** The HeroAvatar is not yet visible. Instead: a real phone screen showing the Arbor avatar-creation interface — the parent is mid-selection, the hero character is taking shape. Then the card appears.

**On-screen text (appears at 0:02, Frank Ruhl Libre, large, centered):**
```
90 שניות. זה מה שלוקח.
```

**EN reference:** "90 seconds. That's all it takes."

**Why this works:** Speed is inherently shareable. "90 שניות" is a concrete, credible, verifiable claim — not a hyperbolic brag. The viewer's disbelief is productive: they watch to see whether it's true.

**Arbor Bar (hook-level check):**
- Memory test: pass at hook level — "90 שניות" refers to creating the personalized artifact.
- Composure test: "זה מה שלוקח" is calm and matter-of-fact. No urgency theater. Pass.
- First-line test: any competitor who does not generate a personalized illustrated hero card cannot run this line. Pass.

**Predicted performance driver:** Brevity claims are among the highest-performing hooks on TikTok IL (validated by Cal AI, Duolingo short-form patterns). "90 שניות" is specific enough to be believable and short enough to survive the first scroll decision.

---

### A-3 — "הוא בכה"
**Type:** RS — the father who was not expecting to be moved.

**Visual (0:00–0:03):** Black frame. Text only — no product UI, no avatar yet. The hook lands as pure text before anything is explained.

**On-screen text (appears at 0:00, Frank Ruhl Libre, large, white on black):**
```
הוא בכה כשראה את עצמו כגיבור.
```

**EN reference:** "He cried when he saw himself as the hero."

**Why this works:** Emotion-first hooks on IL parenting content consistently outperform feature-demonstration hooks in the recognition segment (source: organic reading of IL parent FB/TikTok engagement patterns — not a controlled study). The specific detail ("בכה" — cried) is more powerful than a general emotion word because it is concrete and slightly surprising. A child crying because they saw themselves as a hero is a real, recognizable moment. The hook works silently (WhatsApp preview, muted autoplay) because the text carries it.

**Arbor Bar (hook-level check):**
- Composure test: "הוא בכה" is an observation, not urgency theater. The emotion is the child's, not engineered fear. Pass.
- Enemy test: implicit — no generic app makes a child cry in recognition of themselves. Pass.
- 11pm test: reading this at 11pm does not raise anxiety. It creates warmth. Pass.

**Variation for A/B:** "היא בכתה" (she cried) — test gender split in the IL audience to determine which resonates more in the first 24h.

### A-4 — "שלחה לקבוצת הגן. 14 אמהות ביקשו את הלינק."
**Type:** AS (Authority Stat) — social proof stated as a flat fact.

**Visual (0:00–0:03):** A screenshot of a WhatsApp group — generic, no real names or faces visible, just the avatar image appearing in a chat thread and a row of heart reactions below it.

**On-screen text (appears at 0:01, Heebo, white):**
```
שלחה לקבוצת הגן.
```
*second line fades in at 0:02:*
```
14 אמהות ביקשו את הלינק.
```

**EN reference:** "She shared it in the class group. 14 mothers asked for the link."

**Why this works:** This is the K-factor made visible. The specific number (14) makes it credible — a round number like "dozens" signals fabrication. The WhatsApp group visual is the most recognized digital environment for Israeli parents; it is the platform where viral content spreads in IL, and leading with its visual triggers immediate recognition.

**Arbor Bar (hook-level check):**
- Memory test: the mechanism (personalized avatar → group share → mass interest) only works because the artifact is personalized. Pass.
- Composure test: stating a fact calmly is not urgency theater. "14 אמהות ביקשו" is an observation, not a brag. Pass.
- First-line test: "14 אמהות ביקשו את הלינק" — could this run on Kinedu? Only if Kinedu's artifact was share-worthy enough to trigger this response. It is not. Pass.

**Production note:** The number "14" must match the actual data if this hook is used post-launch with real K-factor data. Pre-launch, use the number from an actual beta-period share event if one occurred; do not fabricate. If no real share event has generated this data, use A-1 or A-3 instead for the launch week. Confirm with arbor-acquisition before producing this hook.

### A-5 — Contrarian correction variant
**Type:** CC (Contrarian Correction) — addresses the parent who is skeptical of "AI for kids."

**Visual (0:00–0:03):** The HeroAvatar is on screen but slightly out of focus / partially visible. The hook arrives as a contrarian statement before the visual fully reveals.

**On-screen text (appears at 0:00, Frank Ruhl Libre, white):**
```
זה לא בינה מלאכותית שמנסה להחליף אותך.
```
*second line at 0:02:*
```
זה הילד שלך. כגיבור.
```

**EN reference:** "This isn't AI trying to replace you. This is your child. As a hero."

**Why this works:** Israeli parents in 2026 are AI-saturated and appropriately skeptical of "AI parenting" pitches. The contrarian opening names the concern before they voice it — and then pivots immediately to the concrete visual. The hook does not defend AI; it dissolves the objection by reframing what the artifact is.

**Arbor Bar (hook-level check):**
- Composure test: naming the skepticism is not urgency theater — it is the opposite. Pass.
- Enemy test: the enemy here is the colonizing-companion AI bot (BRAND-STRATEGY §2, the fourth villain). "לא בינה מלאכותית שמנסה להחליף אותך" names it and dismisses it in one line. Pass.
- Clinician test: a senior child psychologist would approve of the framing "this is the parent's tool, not a replacement." Pass.
- Note: "בינה מלאכותית" (AI) is on the approved list only as a contextual denial ("this is not AI replacing you"), not as a brag ("AI-powered"). This usage is correct. Confirm with arbor-brand before using.

### A-6 — The silent hook (visual-only, no text overlay)
**Type:** RS — for TikTok IL autoplay where the first frame must stop the scroll with no text.

**Visual (0:00–0:03):** The HeroAvatar fills the full 9:16 frame, illustrated, the child clearly the hero of something. No text, no product name, no brand element. Just the character.

**On-screen text:** None in the first 3 seconds. Text arrives at second 4: "הגיבור/ה של [שם הילד]. ב-90 שניות."

**EN reference:** "N/A — the visual is the hook. No translation in the first 3 seconds."

**Why this works:** On TikTok IL, the most effective hooks are sometimes the ones that show without telling — particularly when the visual is strong enough to generate "what is this?" before any copy arrives. The illustrated child-as-hero avatar in a compelling pose is the hook. The viewer stays for the reveal. This variant requires the highest quality avatar illustration — a generic or low-quality hero image will not hold the scroll.

**Arbor Bar (hook-level check):**
- Composure test: no text = no urgency. Pass.
- Production requirement: the hero avatar illustration used in this hook must be production-quality, not a draft render. If the marketing sample avatar is not yet at production quality, use A-1 instead.

## BLOCK B — 2am / "Fear-Google" Hooks

The visual for all Block B hooks: the first frame establishes the 2am context — a phone screen glowing in a dark room, or a parent's face lit by a phone in the dark. The enemy (the fear-Google / the generic search) is named or implied within the first 3 seconds. The product (Arbor) is not named until at least second 4.

---

### B-1 — "שתיים בלילה"
**Type:** RS — the most recognized scenario in IL parent culture.

**Visual (0:00–0:03):** Extreme close-up of a phone screen in a dark room. Time reads 2:17. The parent's hand is visible. Nothing else.

**On-screen text (appears at 0:02, Frank Ruhl Libre, white):**
```
שתיים בלילה.
```

**EN reference:** "2am."

**Why this works:** Two words. The recognized scenario is immediate and total — every parent who has Googled a child's symptom at 2am knows this frame in their body. The hook works silently (muted autoplay) because the image carries it. The text confirms what the image already says.

**Arbor Bar (hook-level check):**
- Enemy test: the 2am fear-Google is the first villain in BRAND-STRATEGY §2. This hook places the viewer inside that moment before naming the enemy. Pass.
- 11pm test: "שתיים בלילה" at 11pm raises recognition, not anxiety — the parent sees themselves, not a warning. Pass.
- Composure test: two words, calm. Pass.

**Predicted performance driver:** This is the simplest hook in the batch. It has the highest chance of both failing (if the visual does not land) and succeeding (if it does) — because simplicity amplifies both directions. The visual quality of the first frame (the phone glow in the dark) is everything.

---

### B-2 — "האינטרנט לא מכיר את הילד שלך"
**Type:** CC — the enemy named in one line.

**Visual (0:00–0:03):** A real Google search results page fills the screen — generic "is this normal" results, ten blue links to parenting sites that have never met this parent's child. The results feel cold and crowded.

**On-screen text (appears at 0:01, Heebo, white, over the Google results):**
```
האינטרנט לא מכיר את הילד שלך.
```

**EN reference:** "The internet doesn't know your child."

**Why this works:** The visual is the enemy — the viewer sees the cold, generic search results before the text arrives, so the text lands as a confirmation of what they already feel. The hook is the fastest possible version of the BRAND-STRATEGY §2 enemy kill: names the 2am fear-Google villain in the first second, delivers the counter-claim in the overlay.

**Arbor Bar (hook-level check):**
- Enemy test: names the first villain explicitly. Pass.
- First-line test: "האינטרנט לא מכיר את הילד שלך" — cannot run on any competitor who also relies on generic content or generic LLM responses. It only passes the one-word-swap test for Arbor because the longitudinal record is what makes the alternative true. Pass.
- Composure test: the tone is calm statement-of-fact, not an attack. Pass.

**Production note:** The Google results page shown on screen must be a real search result — not a mockup. Film a real phone searching "is this normal for toddler" (EN) or "האם זה נורמלי ש" (HE). If the first autocomplete or first result happens to be Arbor-competitive, reshoot with a different query. The point is the generic mass of strangers' results, not any specific result.

## A/B Testing Protocol

**Pre-launch (Days -7 to -1):** Post A-1 and B-1 as IG Stories (organic, no paid). Read 3-second completion rate (IG Stories shows this natively as "swipe-away" rate). A hook with >50% swipe-away in the first 3 seconds is weak — replace with the next variant.

**Launch week (Days 1–7):** Post the top-performing hook from the pre-launch read as the opener for Video 1 (2am concept) and Video 2 (avatar challenge). The remaining hooks become re-cut variants if either video beats baseline.

**Kill rule (from VIRAL-ENGINE.md):** Any hook below 40% intro-retention on TikTok or below 30% swipe-completion on IG Stories is killed after 24 hours. Do not keep a weak hook running out of creative attachment.

**Breakout protocol:** If any post with one of these hooks beats the baseline intro-retention threshold (>70%), re-cut that hook into 3–5 new variants within 48 hours and re-queue. The batch is not exhausted — it is the starting inventory. The breakout derivative is the work.
