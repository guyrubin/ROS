### 1A. ON-CARD COPY (THE HERO COMIC CARD)

The card is the thing the parent shares. Two compositional zones:

**Zone 1 — comic world (no text; child avatar dominates)**
No copy in this zone. The illustrated hero panel renders here. Zero marketing language on the visual — the brand bar sits below it only.

**Zone 2 — the brand bar (bottom strip, sage paper, Frank Ruhl Libre / Heebo)**

HE (RTL, primary):
```
הגיבור/ה של [שם הילד] — ארבור
```

EN (LTR, parallel):
```
[Child name]'s hero — Arbor
```

*[Dynamic: [שם הילד] / [Child name] pulled from the parent's child profile at export. The bar is the only brand presence on the card. No CTA, no URL, no hashtag on the card itself — these live in the caption only. The referral deep-link is embedded in the image file's share payload, not visible on screen.]*

**Design note:** the brand bar must read as the comic's title card, not an ad. Frank Ruhl Libre at 18px. One line. The rest of the card is the child's world.

### 1B. WHATSAPP SHARE CAPTION — FIVE PASTE-READY OPTIONS (HE PRIMARY)

These are the captions the parent taps to send after choosing to share the Hero Comic. They appear in the in-app share sheet as selectable options. The parent selects one, edits the child's name if needed, and sends. Each is under 160 characters. Each carries the deep-link via the share payload, not in the caption text itself (IL parents do not click raw URLs in group captions — the preview card handles it).

**Caption A — Pure delight (recommended default, tone: "this is so us")**
```
הנה [שם הילד] כגיבור. לקח 90 שניות. 🦸
```
EN parallel:
```
Meet [name] — their hero, built in 90 seconds. 🦸
```

**Caption B — Wonder (tone: "I didn't expect this")**
```
לא ציפיתי שזה יוצא כזה. הפרצוף שלו כשראה את זה...
```
EN parallel:
```
Didn't expect it to come out like this. The look on their face when they saw it.
```

**Caption C — The second half (tone: "the comic was the hook, the answer kept me")**
```
שיתפתי בשביל הקומיקס. נשארתי בשביל התשובה שקיבלתי לשאלה שהייתה לי. שווה לנסות.
```
EN parallel:
```
I shared it for the comic. I stayed for the answer to the thing that's been on my mind. Worth trying.
```
*arbor-safety note: "the answer to the question I had" is non-diagnostic. Safe. Confirm before publish.*

**Caption D — Minimal (for parents who do not caption — just the image)**
```
[שם הילד] 🦸 #הגיבורשלי
```
EN parallel:
```
[Name] 🦸 #MyHero
```

**Caption E — For parents who want to explain (longer, ~200 characters)**
```
יצרתי את זה עם ארבור — אפליקציה שבונה קומיקס מהתמונה שלו ועונה על שאלות הורות שיש לי עליו. הגיבור הזה כל כך הוא.
```
EN parallel:
```
Made this with Arbor — it builds a comic from their photo and answers the parenting question I've been carrying. This hero is so them.
```

**In-app share sheet UX note:** present Caption A first (delight, lowest friction). D is the fallback for parents who find any caption too much. Do not show more than three options in the sheet — offer "more options" disclosure for C and E.
