## 2. The innovative concept (the R&D being funded)

A **multimodal, longitudinal developmental-assessment engine** for children 0–6: it ingests everyday, parent-captured signals — speech samples, photos, structured observations, milestone check-ins — and produces (a) a **continuous developmental trajectory** benchmarked to CDC/AAP-2022 and ASHA norms, (b) **red-flag / when-to-refer** detection, and (c) a **personalised intervention plan** delivered through play. The moat is **longitudinal memory**: accuracy compounds per child over months, which point-in-time screeners and generic chatbots cannot do.

**Why it's R&D, not assembly:** turning noisy, consumer-grade home inputs into a **clinically defensible** developmental signal — multimodal fusion (speech + vision + parent report), age/preterm correction, and uncertainty calibration suitable for a *referral* decision — is unproven and is exactly the POC this grant funds.

## 3. The innovation / what's new

- **Longitudinal, multimodal** developmental assessment from home-captured data (vs. one-shot clinic screeners or single-modality apps).
- **Clinical-grade calibration** for a parent-facing product — confidence-aware outputs, conservative escalation, non-pathologising framing.
- **Personalisation loop** — assessment directly drives the next speech/play intervention and re-measures.

## 4. The problem & market

Developmental delays are most treatable when caught early, yet detection is sparse, episodic, and inequitable; parents lack a continuous, trustworthy read on whether their child is on track and what to *do*. Market: a global, multilingual (HE/EN/NL/DE/FR) digital child-development category spanning EdTech, parenting, and preventive child health — large, growing, and underserved on the *clinical-credibility* axis Arbor targets.
