# GTM Netherlands v1 — Arbor

Last updated: 2026-05-15
Status: Phase 0 working draft

---

## Decision

Use the Netherlands as the institutional scaling market, but begin with a small parent concierge beta to prove trust and repeat usage.

Israel can operate in parallel as the expert/content engine. Belgium/EU comes later.

---

## Market Logic

The Netherlands is attractive because family support, youth care, schools, municipalities, insurers, and prevention-oriented services create a B2B2C path. The constraint is trust: institutions will not adopt an AI parenting product without safety, privacy, professional boundaries, and evidence.

Therefore GTM must start as:

1. Parent value validation
2. Professional trust validation
3. Institutional pilot narrative
4. Paid pilot

---

## Customer Segments

### Beachhead: Parents of children 2–8
Need help with behavior, routines, emotional regulation, school readiness, and recurring worries.

### Design partners: Professionals
Parent coaches, child psychologists, speech therapists, educators, pediatric-adjacent practitioners.

### Institutional buyers
Municipalities, schools/daycare networks, insurers, youth/family support organizations.

---

## Revenue Model Tests

1. B2C parent subscription
2. Professional dashboard subscription
3. Institutional pilot fee
4. Premium assessment/report workflow
5. Localized expert-reviewed content packs

Do not start with expert marketplace. Add it only after trust and workflow pull are proven.

---

## First Offer

### Parent beta offer
“Arbor helps you understand what is happening with your child, what to do today, what to track, and when to involve a professional.”

### Professional pilot offer
“Arbor improves intake quality and follow-through by turning parent observations into structured timelines, action plans, and handoff notes.”

### Institutional pilot offer
“Arbor provides scalable early parent support and triage while improving professional handoff and preventing avoidable escalation.”

---

## 90-Day Execution Plan

### Days 1–30

- Create PRD, safety policy, data model, and scenario library.
- Recruit 10–20 parent testers.
- Recruit 2–3 professional reviewers.
- Run concierge responses manually/semi-manually.

### Days 31–60

- Process 100+ parent questions.
- Identify top 5 recurring use cases.
- Create child profile/timeline MVP.
- Generate professional handoff notes.
- Collect usefulness and trust feedback.

### Days 61–90

- Convert concierge workflow into private beta.
- Package Netherlands pilot one-pager.
- Approach first municipality/school/professional organization conversations.
- Decide first paid test.

---

## Risks

- Product sounds like therapy or diagnosis.
- Safety policy is underdeveloped.
- Parents treat AI guidance as medical authority.
- Institutions require more evidence than available.
- B2C retention is weak without routine follow-up.

---

## Next Action

Start with friendly parent testers and professional reviewers before institutional selling.
