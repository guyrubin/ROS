## 0. Why now (the trigger)

The avatar is the headline failure: today Arbor can *barely upload a child photo* (raw file → 256px thumbnail → Storage or inline data URL; `ProfileEditDrawer.tsx` + `lib/image.ts` + `lib/storage.ts`). There is **no AI image generation anywhere in the stack** — the story `imagePrompt` field is generated by the model and then **never rendered**; all illustrations are deterministic SVG. Meanwhile the Practice Studio games are functional but **pre-authored mockups** (local heuristics, Web Speech API, static content), and the growth surfaces (Milestones, Development Copilot, Plans) track and advise but do **not** close the loop. A raw photo upload is *below table stakes* for 2026; the category leaders have moved to stylized characters, child-as-hero generative content, adaptive play, and assess→act→re-assess growth engines.

This PRD turns the avatar into a defensible moat-extension, makes the games best-in-class, and makes Practice + Growth a closed adaptive loop — all built on the one asset competitors can't copy: **Arbor's longitudinal child-memory record**.

---

## 1. Decision

Ship three coordinated workstreams, sequenced so the avatar foundation unlocks the rest:

1. **Augmented Avatar (keystone).** A **privacy-first stylized character** the child *is* across Arbor — built from descriptors by default, optionally from a photo that is **discarded after generation** (never stored, never used for training). Generated with **Gemini 2.5 Flash Image ("Nano Banana")**: character-consistent, ~$0.039/image, SynthID + C2PA watermarked. The character then becomes (a) the **hero of their own Adventures/stories**, (b) an **emotion-state avatar** in FeelingsLab, and (c) earns **development-driven cosmetics**.

2. **Best-in-market Practice Studio.** Real adaptivity and real signal, minus dark patterns: adaptive difficulty (DKT/ZPD) in MemoryMatch, **real kid-tuned phoneme scoring** + ASHA taxonomy/dosage in SpeechCoach, **on-device MediaPipe** geometry scoring in MimicStudio, depicted-emotion labeling in FeelingsLab, **Gemini-generated** branching Adventures with a layered kids-safety guardrail stack, and a **weekly milestone closed-loop** for Missions.

3. **Closed-loop Practice + Growth Plan.** Re-base milestones on the **CDC/AAP-2022 75th-percentile** set + **ASHA-2023** communication/feeding, then ship an **assess → weekly plan → auto-regenerate** Growth Plan (the Kinedu engine, but powered by Arbor's logs) plus a **longitudinal Development Score** and a **JITAI nudge engine** that fires off the child's logged state, not the clock.

**Non-negotiable across all three:** amended **COPPA (full compliance April 22, 2026 — now in force)** classifies **faceprints/voiceprints as personal information** and requires **separate verifiable parental consent** to *process a face* and a *further* consent to *train on it*. Therefore: **no raw face stored, no child data to model training, on-device biometrics wherever possible, explicit consent gates, SynthID/C2PA on outputs.** This is not overhead — it *is* the avatar's defensibility story and Arbor's brand promise ("parent-owned, non-diagnostic, clinically credible").

## 2. Why it wins

- **The avatar is the visual embodiment of the memory spine.** Competitors treat the child as a data record (Lovevery/Kinedu/Huckleberry: name + raw photo, no avatar layer) or hand them a generic mascot (Khan's Kodi, Duolingo). The 2026 frontier — photo → *consistent illustrated character* → child as hero of generated content — is owned today by standalone storybook apps (MyStoryBot, Childbook.ai, StoryBookly). **None of them pairs the consistent hero with a real developmental-memory record.** Arbor's hero is fed by *actual logged moments*, which is non-copyable.
- **The enabling model arrived and is cheap.** Nano Banana does ≤5-subject consistency, 512px→4K, ~4¢/image, with provenance watermarking — no custom diffusion training needed. The cost/safety math finally works for a parenting app.
- **The games' biggest gap is the open market gap.** The two leading speech apps (Speech Blubs, Articulation Station) **don't use real ASR** — Blubs only detects "any vocalization," Articulation Station is self-scored. Real kid-tuned phoneme scoring (SoapBox / fine-tuned Whisper, target-word-scoped) gated by the child's age against ASHA norms is *category-defining*, and Arbor's data moat picks *which* sounds to drill.
- **The growth loop is structurally unique.** Per NFX's data-moat criteria, the defensible asset is *perishable, auto-captured, per-child longitudinal data central to the value*. A content-only rival can copy articles; it **cannot** replicate a specific child's months of logged behavior. Kinedu regenerates plans weekly against milestones; Arbor can regenerate against **milestones + logged behavior + completed-vs-missed practice** — a loop competitors can't run.
- **JITAI is the unclaimed lane.** Cleo/Cooper/Maven are human-coach-led and vague on cadence; the digital BCT literature over-uses clock-based reminders and under-uses context-aware just-in-time cues. Firing nudges off the child's *logged state* (ML send-timing lifts receptivity up to ~40%; cold-start solvable after ~10–20 events) is something no competitor operationalizes.

## 3. Build

### 3.1 Workstream A — Augmented Avatar

**A0. Image-generation backend (foundation).** New provider capability `generateImage()` on `ModelProvider` (Gemini 2.5 Flash Image returns image bytes in `inlineData` parts, *not* JSON — distinct from the existing `generateJson` path). New model env `VERTEX_MODEL_IMAGE` / `GEMINI_IMAGE_MODEL` defaulting to `gemini-2.5-flash-image`. New route **`POST /api/generate-avatar`**: accepts either descriptors (`{ hair, skin, eyes, vibe, style }`) or an optional photo data URL; safety-gated (reuse `screenForImmediateEscalation` + the image guard), quota-gated, size-capped (≤6MB, image MIME only); returns a stylized character data URL. **Raw photo never persisted server-side** — used only as a reference part in the single generation call, then dropped. Style presets: *Storybook, Soft 3D, Watercolor, Flat-cute* (non-photoreal only — never realistic likeness).

**A1. Stylized Character Creator (client).** Replace the bare photo upload in `ProfileEditDrawer` with an **Avatar Creator** drawer/modal: pick path (Describe ▸ default, or Photo ▸ consent-gated), choose style, generate, pick from variants, save. New `ChildProfile.avatar` field (`{ url, style, source: 'descriptor'|'photo', createdAt }`); keep `photoUrl` for back-compat. Render everywhere via the existing `Avatar` component (ProfileSwitcher, Overview, Sidebar). **No-photo path is the default** so the app is fully usable without ever touching a face.

**A2. Consent gate.** Before any face is processed: explicit Safety-L3 parental-consent modal ("Arbor will turn this photo into a cartoon character and immediately delete the original. The photo is never stored and never used to train AI."). Persist consent record. No consent → Photo path disabled, Describe path always available.

**A3. Child-as-hero (fast-follow).** Wire the currently-dead `imagePrompt`: pass the saved character asset as a reference into a scene-generation call so the child's consistent character is the protagonist in Hero Journey/Adventures beats. `HeroScenePlayer` renders the generated scene (fallback to today's SVG/photo). Cost-guard: generate on demand + cache per (story, beat, childAvatar).

**A4. Emotion-state avatar (fast-follow).** A finite, authorable expression set (Bitmoji pattern) driven by FeelingsLab interactions and logged mood — child taps "how I feel," avatar mirrors it. Expressions are pre-rendered per character to control cost.

**A5. Progression cosmetics & memory portraits (later).** Unlockables earned by *doing development activities* (not money, not streaks); milestone-driven "memory portraits" pinned to the timeline; watermarked "hero card" export via existing exporters + redaction (activates Trusted Sharing).

### 3.2 Workstream B — Practice Studio (best-in-market)

Per-module upgrades (all on `practice/` pure-module + content-bank patterns, unit-tested):
- **MemoryMatch** — adaptive difficulty (DKT/DDA → converge card-set to the child's ZPD), profile-themed cards. *Lowest effort, immediate retention proof.*
- **Missions** — replace static challenges with the **Kinedu weekly closed-loop**: generate against milestones not-yet-reached, regenerate weekly from completed-vs-missed. *Pure backend/Gemini on the owned moat.*
- **SpeechCoach** — adopt Articulation Station taxonomy (**22 phonemes × 3 word positions × 4 levels: word→phrase→sentence→story**), add **real phoneme-level scoring** (SoapBox or fine-tuned Whisper, target-word-scoped, on-device-first), gate target sounds by age against **ASHA norms** (plosives/nasals/glides by 3;11 … fricatives 6;11), enforce ASHA dosage (50–100 trials, 2–3×/week). *Highest strategic differentiation.*
- **Adventures** — Gemini-generated branching stories personalized to profile/interests/current milestone targets, behind the layered kids-safety guardrail stack (in-prompt + input-threat detection + async moderation + human-in-loop; adult keyword filters are documented-inadequate for kids).
- **MimicStudio** — on-device **MediaPipe blendshapes/pose** (52 blendshape coefficients), score **geometry match only** (mimic, never diagnose). Privacy-clean.
- **FeelingsLab** — child labels a **depicted** emotion (never infers the child's own feeling — a 2025 emotion-detection study on autistic children was *retracted* over validity/ethics); Gemini generates scenario variety tied to SEL milestones.

**Cross-cutting (mandatory):** strip any streaks / variable-reward / autoplay / asymmetric dark patterns (UK AADC + Fairplay/FTC explicitly name these as manipulative for minors); design **short bursts with clean stopping points** and **co-play prompts** (AAP-2026 dropped fixed time limits → quality + family-time framing); keep all biometric processing **on-device** (COPPA).

### 3.3 Workstream C — Practice + Growth Plan (closed loop)

- **C1. Re-base milestones** on the **CDC/AAP-2022 75th-percentile** set (159 milestones / 12 checklists mapped to well-child visits incl. 15- & 30-mo) + **ASHA-2023** communication & feeding (birth–5; ASHA uniquely adds feeding/swallowing). Add a photo/video "what the skill looks like" per milestone and **corrected-age** support. *P0, credibility signal.*
- **C2. Closed-loop Growth Plan** — assessment → weekly plan of evidence-based activities → **auto-regenerate from completed-vs-missed** (Kinedu engine, Arbor logs). Anchor activity mechanisms in **Triple P / Incredible Years / Tuning-in-to-Kids** (goal-set → model → practice → feedback → reinforce → home practice). *P0.*
- **C3. JITAI nudge engine** — trigger practice/coaching prompts off the child's **logged behavioral state**; group-level cold-start → personalize after ~10–20 events; ML-timed sends. *P0, the wedge no competitor runs.*
- **C4. Longitudinal Development Score** per domain with trend lines (Nanit/SweetSpot pattern: age baseline + personalize from the child's own ≥5-day history → one number + next action). *P1, the moat made visible.*
- **C5. Safe red-flag layer** — surface ASQ-3-style monitoring-zone signals + Pathways red flags as **"discuss with your provider"** + auto-generated provider PDF (reuse handoff exporter). Surveillance, **not** diagnosis. *P1.*
- **C6. COPPA-2026 consent granularity** — separate share-consent and AI-training-consent, retention limits, provable deletion. *P0 legal gate, blocks any face/voice/training feature.*

## 5. Success metrics

- **Avatar adoption:** % of active children with a generated character (target ≥60% within 30 days of ship); % choosing Describe vs Photo path (privacy signal).
- **Retention (the H1 kill-metric):** D7/D30 return rate; child-as-hero story completions per active child.
- **Practice quality:** SpeechCoach trials/week vs ASHA dosage target; MemoryMatch ZPD-convergence time (<1 hr).
- **Growth loop:** % of children with an active auto-regenerating plan; weekly plan completion rate; JITAI nudge receptivity (vs clock-based baseline).
- **Trust/safety:** zero raw-face persistence (audited); 100% consent coverage before any face processing; SynthID/C2PA present on all generated images.

---

## 6. Risks

- **Cost runaway on image gen** → per-call quota (reuse `aiQuota`), cache generated scenes per (story, beat, avatar), Plus-gate high-volume generation, ~4¢/image budget tracked.
- **Privacy/regulatory** → the whole no-raw-face / no-train / on-device / consent architecture is the mitigation; it is *load-bearing*, ship it with the avatar, not after.
- **Cold-start / sparse data** (Growth Score, JITAI, adaptivity) → age-band priors + honest low-confidence states; never invent precision.
- **Kid-ASR accuracy** (off-the-shelf Whisper ~25% WER on kids vs ~3% adults) → scope to target-word drills with a known reference (tractable), on-device-first, fall back to record-and-playback (the incumbent UX) when confidence is low.
- **Dark-pattern drift** → product-review gate against AADC/Fairplay list before any "engagement" feature ships.
- **Don't over-claim durability** — digital BCT evidence is overwhelmingly <3 months; instrument long-term retention, market honestly.

## 8. UPDATE (2026-06-17b) — Best-in-market capability mining + the Cross-Domain Hero & Comic Identity

**Trigger:** the avatar must not be a profile picture — it must be the child rendered as the **hero of the whole platform** (the reference: premium cel-shaded comic/superhero art, the child caped and mid-action with KA-POW! SFX). And the games must visibly out-deliver the three category leaders parents already know: **Khan Academy Kids, Lingokids, Duolingo ABC.**

### 8.1 Capability mining → Arbor, delivered better

Every leader hands the child a **generic mascot** and a **fixed curriculum**. Arbor's two structural advantages — **(1) the child IS the hero** (their own generated character) and **(2) the path is driven by the child's real logged development** — are non-copyable. Map:

|---|---|---|
| **Khan Kids** · guided cast (Kodi + friends) | a character makes it feel personal & safe | The child's **own Hero Avatar** guides — they aren't *with* a character, they *are* the character |
| **Khan Kids** · adaptive Learning Path + open Library | structure + autonomy | The **closed-loop Growth Plan** (Workstream C) — driven by the child's logged development, not a static syllabus |
| **Khan Kids** · creativity studio (draw/color/sing); no ads, no fail states | open-ended, pressure-free | **Hero Comic / Story generation** starring the child; anti-dark-pattern by charter (AADC/Fairplay), free core |

### 8.2 The Cross-Domain Hero Avatar — definition (build this)

**Principle: one avatar, generated once, the child IS it everywhere.** Not a per-screen picture — a single cross-domain identity asset.

- **Data model (exists):** `ChildProfile.photoUrl` (image) + `ChildProfile.avatar { url, style, source, createdAt }`. Add style `"comichero"`. Treat the avatar as first-class; expose via a shared `HeroAvatar` accessor with a deterministic **Sprout fallback** when none exists yet.
- **IA placement (where the hero appears):**
  - **Today/Home** — the hero greets ("Time to play, {name}?") instead of the generic Sprout once created.
  - **Practice Studio** — the hero is the player-character in every game header + celebrations; **Adventures** casts the hero as protagonist; **FeelingsLab** uses the hero as the emotion-state avatar.
  - **Academy** — the hero stars in **Story Journeys** (child-as-hero comic/story) and fronts Masterclass/Family cards as "your child's guide."
  - **My Child / Development** — anchors the profile, milestone "memory portraits," and the Development Score.
  - **Care / Sharing** — a watermarked "hero card" export.
- **Generation flow (exists):** `AvatarCreator` (Describe default / Photo consent-gated) → `/api/generate-avatar` → stylized character saved to profile → rendered everywhere. Wherever the fallback shows, surface a **"Create {name}'s hero"** CTA so the cross-domain upgrade is one tap from any domain.

### 8.3 Comic Hero visual identity + the Hero Comic generator (AI capability)

- **New avatar style preset "Comic Hero"** — caped superhero, cel-shaded, dynamic lines, bright primary palette (the reference). Added to `/api/generate-avatar` style set + the client picker.
- **Hero Comic generator** — a personalized full-page comic panel starring the child's hero (optionally a sibling), built on **Nano Banana (Gemini 2.5 Flash Image)**, provenance-watermarked, consent-gated, cached per (story, beat, avatar). The user-supplied **comic-page spec is the canonical prompt template**: single dynamic cel-shaded full-page panel; cast = child hero (+ sibling) in matching suits; personalized home setting; verbatim SFX (KA-POW! / ZAP! / AWOO! / WOOF!) and dialogue slots filled from the story beat. Surfaced as a **"Hero Comic"** content type in Academy → Story Journeys and as Adventures scene art (wires the currently-dead `imagePrompt`, PRD §A3).
- **Comic energy as moments, not wallpaper** — action-line/SFX accents on celebrations and hero reveals only, inside the token system (anti-overstimulation, AADC).
