## 0. The binding frame (read first)

- **The 0–24m engine is real and mostly already shipped.** Unlike the school-age band (where CDC stops at age 5), the baby band has **authoritative discrete-milestone science**: `src/lib/milestoneData.ts` already carries **CDC-2022 milestones 2–24m** (Zubler et al., *Pediatrics* 2022;149(3):e2021052138) + **ASHA** feeding/babbling/communication rows; `correctedAgeMonths()` (`src/lib/childAge.ts`) implements **AAP preterm correction**; `monitoring.ts` is the non-diagnostic watch engine; `growth/growthEntries.ts` stores append-only height/weight/head-circumference and **deliberately ships NO percentile reference table.** So this band's work is *what data/reference we add and in what register* + *honest reassurance* — not new intelligence. **B0 and B1 are already shipped green** and confirmed clinically sound by the peds + SLP lenses below.
- **The CDC-2022 75th-percentile design is the band's safety asset.** The revision moved to *"the age by which **most** children can…"* (75th-percentile) and added 15- and 30-month checkpoints. This **removes the false-alarm "your child is behind" framing** by design — a milestone not yet checked is a *cue to ask*, not a deficit. **Binding copy rule for the whole band: "most babies can… by [age]," never "your baby should…"**
- **Surveillance, never diagnosis.** Every baby surface surfaces an **observable behavior or a parent-logged measurement**, never a **condition**. Banned as a verdict/output at every baby surface: "delayed," "delay," "behind," "below average," "disorder," "abnormal," "at risk for," "autism/autistic," "ADHD," "CP," "speech delay" (as a verdict about *this* baby), "hearing loss/deaf," "dysphagia," "aspiration," "feeding disorder," "sleep disorder," "failure to thrive," "faltering/growth faltering," "underweight," "stunted," "wasting." These are clinician determinations made with direct evaluation — **not Arbor outputs.**
- **G2 (claims).** Mechanism cited, never an effect size, never "proven/validated/clinically validated/clinically accurate." "Evidence-informed surveillance" is the ceiling. No claim that any Arbor activity improves/trains/accelerates a baby's development.
- **Firewall (advisory.md §0).** All copy is **"developmentally informed, grounded in CDC/AAP/ASHA/WHO"** — never "clinically validated," "clinician-reviewed," or "clinician-approved." No adviser/thinker identity anywhere. Arbor's reviewers are not licensed clinicians and are never presented as such.
- **Parent-mediated is the developmentally-correct design here, not just brand.** A 0–24m child *cannot operate an app*, so **every** baby surface addresses the **parent** (the data controller); the pediatrician/SLP owns clinical interpretation. The psych lens grounds this: infant regulation is *external and dyadic* (co-regulation precedes self-regulation; Bowlby/Ainsworth secure base) — an app that inserts itself between infant and caregiver as a soother works *against* the mechanism. This is the **easiest band to keep parent-mediated** (no child-facing UI for a baby), which makes any drift conspicuous and a **recommend-veto** (see §4).
- **EU-MDR read** is required before any **percentile/reference-overlay as a result**, any **inference of a condition from logs** (feeding/sleep/swallowing), and the whole **B2 growth-interpretation** surface ships — surfacing a measurement against a norm to flag a possible condition sits near the medical-device boundary (MDR Annex VIII Rule 11 / MDCG software-qualification). That read is the lead's call, flagged per item below.

## B2 — Growth percentiles (weight / length / head-circumference) *(the headline gated item)*
*(lens: peds, lead)*

**Requirement (one line).** Arbor may show the parent-logged **longitudinal trajectory** (and, only if it ever overlays a reference, plot it on the **WHO 0–24m / CDC 2y+** convention against **corrected age** for preterm, as a labeled *reference band, not a result*) framed as **context to bring to the pediatrician** — but must **never** output a precise percentile-as-result, a growth verdict, or a centile-crossing alarm.

**Mechanism + source (mechanism only, no effect size).**
- **The reference is not one table — it is a WHO-under-2 / CDC-over-2 split.** CDC + AAP recommend U.S. providers use the **WHO Child Growth Standards birth–24 months** (weight-for-age, length-for-age, weight-for-length, head-circumference-for-age) to monitor growth *regardless of feeding type*, then **CDC growth reference charts at 2 years and older.** The WHO charts are a **prescriptive standard** (a breastfed reference population describing how children grow under optimal conditions); the CDC charts are a **descriptive reference.** A B2 reference therefore transitions at **24 months.** (CDC *Growth Chart Training — Recommendations and Rationale*; CDC MMWR RR-59(9) *Use of WHO and CDC Growth Charts for Children Aged 0–59 Months*; AAP News *CDC: Use WHO growth charts for children under 2*.)
- **Preterm:** plot all parameters on **corrected (adjusted) age** — age from the *due date* — to avoid systematically misclassifying a preterm infant as undergrown during catch-up. (AAP HealthyChildren *Corrected Age for Preemies*; AAP Pediatrics 2018 *Monitoring the Postnatal Growth of Preterm Infants*.)
- **Surveillance-vs-measurement:** a single reading is a **measurement**; growth *assessment* is **surveillance over time.** The defensible point Arbor mirrors: **a single percentile is far less meaningful than whether the child tracks their own curve over time**, and **interpreting a curve crossing centiles is a clinician judgment** (depends on feeding, illness, mid-parental size, measurement error). (AAP/Nationwide Children's *Growth Charts for Premature Babies: Following Their Own Curve*.)

**Surveillance-not-diagnosis + when-to-refer (non-diagnostic register).** Arbor's role ends at *"here is your child's own trajectory over time; bring it to your next well-child visit."* The when-to-refer line stays the **pediatrician's**, surfaced as a *prompt to the parent*, never an Arbor verdict — e.g. *"Your pediatrician plots these on a standard growth chart and looks at the pattern over time. That's the right place to ask whether [child]'s growth is on track for them."* **The current `growthEntries.ts` stance — append-only, no embedded reference table, "fabricating reference curves would be worse than showing none" — is clinically correct and should be preserved.** Do not pressure B2 into shipping the percentile number to close a perceived "feature gap."

**Status — split:**
- **Substantiated (may ship):** the raw longitudinal trajectory (height/weight/head-circ over time, already built); plain "context for your pediatrician" framing; *optionally* a faint **reference band / shaded normal range as visual context** IF AND ONLY IF it is sourced WHO-0–24m / CDC-2y+, plotted on corrected age for preterm, labeled a *reference range* (not a result), and copy still routes interpretation to the pediatrician.
- **GATED — HELD (EU-MDR read + recommend-veto if unhedged):** (a) a **precise percentile-as-result** ("[child] is at the 4th percentile"); (b) **any verdict string** — `underweight`, `overweight`, `failure to thrive`, `faltering growth`, `growth faltering`, `stunted`, `wasting`, `poor weight gain`, `concerning`; (c) any **centile-crossing alarm** — "dropping centiles," "crossing two major centile lines," "growth is slowing." These convert a measurement aid into a **clinical conclusion** (the exact false-alarm/false-reassurance failure the board vetoes) and push the surface into **EU-MDR** territory (interpretation of measurements for a medical purpose). Route any of these to the MDR read before build-ready.

**⚠ Carry-forward for the council (peds lens, important):** the **preterm growth-correction window is NOT the 24-month milestone ceiling.** B0's `CORRECTION_CEILING_MONTHS = 24` is correct for the *developmental* engine, but public growth guidance commonly corrects age **beyond 24 months** (into the 2–3y range), and head-circumference vs weight/length can stop correcting at different points. **If B2 ever plots against a reference, spec a separate correction parameter — do not import `CORRECTION_CEILING_MONTHS`.**

**cited_basis:** CDC *Growth Chart Training — Recommendations and Rationale*; CDC MMWR RR-59(9) (2010); AAP News *CDC: Use WHO growth charts for children under 2*; AAP HealthyChildren *Corrected Age for Preemies*; AAP/Nationwide Children's *Growth Charts for Premature Babies: Following Their Own Curve*; AAP Pediatrics 2018 *Monitoring the Postnatal Growth of Preterm Infants* (e20172467); WHO Child Growth Standards (0–24m).
**Honesty flag:** the WHO/CDC convention and corrected-age plotting are settled; the *interpretation* of any individual curve is clinician judgment, not an Arbor output — copy must keep that line. **HE-translation-sensitive:** *percentile* (אחוזון), *failure to thrive / faltering growth* (the Hebrew clinical term **"כשל שגשוג"** carries full diagnostic weight — must NOT appear in parent copy), *corrected/adjusted age* ("גיל מתוקן"). HE flagged for **human** translation.

## B3 — Daily rhythm log (feeds / sleep / diapers)
*(lens: psych + slp, lead)*

**Requirement (one line).** B3 is **safe as neutral parent self-logging folded into the longitudinal record and reflected back as the family's *own observed pattern*** — with zero norm comparison, zero sleep-training nudge, and zero inference of a feeding/sleep/swallowing condition.

**Mechanism + source (mechanism only, no effect size).**
- **Psych / attachment:** responsive, contingent caregiving (reading and answering the infant's own signals) is the route to secure attachment — the caregiver as a predictable **secure base / safe haven** (Bowlby; Ainsworth, *Patterns of Attachment*, 1978, maternal sensitivity). A log that mirrors the baby's *own* rhythm supports sensitivity; a log that ranks the baby against a population norm *undermines* it by redirecting attention from the baby to a chart. **AAP sleep figures are ranges, not targets** (AAP-endorsed consensus, Paruthi et al., *J Clin Sleep Med* 2016); AAP Bright Futures frames infant sleep/feeding as wide normal variation.
- **SLP / feeding:** pediatric feeding/swallowing (dysphagia) is an **ASHA SLP clinical domain requiring direct evaluation**, not log inference. ASHA's signs (coughing/gagging/choking at meals, arching/stiffening, refusal, wet/gurgly voice after eating, nasal regurgitation, breathing difficulty while feeding, poor weight gain, lengthy meals) are *contact-your-pediatrician* cues — never an app's self-assessment. (ASHA *Feeding and Swallowing Disorders in Children*.)

**Non-pathologizing framing + when-to-refer.** Reflect the parent's *own* trend, never a verdict ("Over the last 10 days, [name]'s longest stretch has been clustering in the early evening" — descriptive, child-as-own-baseline). The when-it's-worth-a-conversation line is a **routing prompt, not a finding**: a *marked, persistent change from the baby's own baseline* (persistent feeding refusal, sustained drop in wet diapers, a new and lasting shift in settling; the ASHA feeding-red-flag cues) → *"worth raising with your pediatrician, who can refer you to a feeding/swallowing specialist (SLP)."* Attachment-safe stance is **no-parent-blame** — copy never implies a pattern is the parent's fault or that they are "doing it wrong." (Acute safety strings already governed by the product CLAUDE.md triggers — fewer wet diapers / breathing / lethargy → urgent — are *safety routing*, kept intact, not a developmental claim.)

**Status — split:**
- **Substantiated (safe to ship):** neutral entry of feeds (breast/bottle/volume/solids introduced) / sleep / diapers in the parent's own words; folding into the longitudinal record; descriptive reflection of the *family's own* trend with explicit uncertainty (inherit the `rhythm/predict.ts` honest-confidence contract — bands not minutes, a "still learning, N more days" low/none state).
- **GATED — HELD (child-data consent [Guy/`arbor-safety`, not the board's to clear] + EU-MDR on any inference):** any comparison of the baby's totals to a **population norm**; any **"X hours" target**; any **sleep-training** method/schedule; any **feeding-adequacy** judgement; any inference of a **feeding/swallowing/sleep disorder** from log patterns; any **predictive** claim ("if this continues, [name] will…"). Comparing a logged physiological metric to a norm to flag a possible condition is the inference/diagnosis-support function MDR Rule 11 pulls toward a device claim.
- **Banned strings:** "your baby should sleep…", "X hours of sleep," "sleep training / cry-it-out / Ferber / self-soothe on a schedule," "sleep problem," "sleep disorder," "feeding disorder," "dysphagia," "aspiration," "your baby should eat/drink…", "not eating enough," "abnormal feeding," "oral-motor delay," "reflux" (as a verdict), "behind / ahead," "below/above average."

**Design note (code-grounded).** `rhythm/predict.ts` already models the right discipline (bands, honest `confidence: none/low`, no invented precision) — B3 must inherit it. The live risk is `jitai.ts`: the CALM wind-down nudge is acceptable as a *gentle suggestion* ("evenings often go smoother when wind-down starts early") but for 0–24m must **never** harden into an infant bedtime *prescription* ("[name] should be asleep by 7").

**cited_basis:** Bowlby, *Attachment and Loss*; Ainsworth et al., *Patterns of Attachment* (1978); AAP Bright Futures (infant sleep/feeding anticipatory guidance); AAP-endorsed sleep consensus (Paruthi et al., *J Clin Sleep Med* 2016 — ranges not prescriptions); ASHA *Feeding and Swallowing Disorders in Children* / *Pediatric Feeding and Swallowing* practice portal.
**Procedural note (not the board's to clear):** B3 captures new child-health data → **consent / COPPA-2026 / GDPR** is a Guy/`arbor-safety` gate, parent = named data controller. This packet clears only clinical soundness/claims.
**Honesty flag:** confidence is *medium* on the exact MDR line for B3's norm-comparison — recommend the regulatory pass confirm the Rule 11 read before any norm-comparison feature is greenlit. **HE-translation-sensitive:** "sleep training" / "disorder" have no neutral casual Hebrew equivalent (both pull clinical/values-laden) — avoid per the bans; "worth raising with your pediatrician" must stay *suggestive* (כדאי להעלות), never imperative.

## B4 — "A leap is coming" reassurance
*(lens: psych + slp, lead)*

**Requirement (one line).** Ship the **attachment-safe normalization** version — *"fussy/clingy/regressed-sleep stretches are common and usually pass; stay a steady, responsive presence"* — grounded in **normal developmental variability**, and explicitly **NOT** a Wonder-Weeks-branded leap calendar or any fixed week-N prediction.

**Mechanism + source (mechanism only, no effect size).** Normal development is **uneven and non-linear**; periods of disorganization, extra clinginess, and temporary sleep/feeding disruption are part of typical maturation. AAP **Bright Futures anticipatory guidance** exists precisely to *normalize behavioral variation* ahead of time so parents don't over-read it. CDC frames milestones as the age by which *most* children show a skill — wide individual variation is expected (Zubler et al., *Pediatrics* 2022, already in `milestoneData.ts`). The protective factor through a wobble is a **maintained responsive presence** — the parent as a stable secure base (Bowlby/Ainsworth).

**Honesty flag on the founder's ask (binding).** The **Wonder Weeks fixed 10-leap calendar is NOT evidence-based** — the original "regression weeks" finding has not replicated and the precise-week predictions are treated with caution by pediatric reviewers. Arbor may deliver the *reassurance the founder wants* but **must not** deliver it via a deterministic calendar. **Take the mechanism (wobbles are normal, they pass, stay responsive); reject the false precision (week-N) and the branding.**

**Non-pathologizing framing + when-to-refer.** Universal + transient ("Many babies go through stretches of extra fussiness or clinginess — common, and usually passes"), never deterministic about *this* baby on *this* week. Coach the parent role, not a fix (stay responsive, keep routines steady, lower the bar for the week — competence/responsibility, not dependence). When-to-refer: if a stretch involves **loss of previously acquired skills**, comes with illness signs, or is marked and *not* passing → pediatrician. **"Leaps are normal" must never soft-pedal a genuine regression** — skill loss is a real red flag the peds lens owns (see §4 / Surveillance refinement).

**Status — split:**
- **Substantiated (ships):** generic, non-dated normalization that fussy/clingy/uneven-progress phases are common and usually pass, tied to the child's *age band* (not a week number); "stay responsive, keep routines steady" coaching; the loss-of-skill / illness referral off-ramp.
- **GATED — HELD + recommend-veto:** any **fixed leap-week calendar** ("leap 5 at week 26"); any **"Wonder Weeks"/"leaps" branding**; any **predictive** claim that *this* baby will be fussy/regress at week N; any claim a leap *causes* a developmental jump (effect-size/causal claim). HELD as both an unverified developmental claim (board veto per advisory.md §2) and a third-party-IP/branding exposure.
- **Banned strings:** "Wonder Weeks / wonder week," "leap" as a dated event ("leap 4 starts week 26"), "your baby will be fussy/difficult this week," "[name] is in leap 5," "mental leap," "developmental leap will make [name]…", any week-numbered fussiness calendar. ("A stretch may feel harder sometimes" is fine.)

**cited_basis:** AAP Bright Futures (anticipatory guidance normalizing behavioral variation); Zubler et al., *Pediatrics* 2022;149(3):e2021052138 (75th-percentile, wide normal variation); Bowlby/Ainsworth (responsive secure base through disruption); Wonder Weeks "regression weeks" — non-replicated / not evidence-based (basis for rejecting the calendar).
**Honesty flag:** confidence *high* on the Wonder-Weeks non-validation and the normal-variability mechanism — ship the sentiment, never the schedule. **HE-translation-sensitive:** "leap" must **not** translate as a branded/dated קפיצה event — use phase language (שלב חולף); "usually passes" must avoid a guarantee register ("בטוח יעבור"). HE flagged for **human** translation.

## Baby surveillance refinement (0–24m) — red-flag / when-to-refer
*(lens: peds + slp, lead)*

**Requirement (one line).** Keep all red-flag/referral logic in the CDC **"Learn the Signs. Act Early."** *"act early — talk with your child's doctor"* register, mirror **"most babies can…"** never **"your baby should…"**, and **add a missing regression signal**.

**Mechanism + source.** Adopt the CDC LTSAE referral trigger in register: *"If your child is not meeting one or more milestones, has lost skills he or she once had, or you have other concerns, act early — talk with your child's doctor."* Two requirements:
1. **Loss of skills at any age = act-early referral *(NEW — gap found).*** `monitoring.ts` flags *unobserved* milestones past-band but does **not** detect **regression** (a previously `checked:true` skill the parent un-checks, or a logged skill loss). CDC names **loss of previously acquired skills at any age** as an explicit act-early trigger. **Add a calm regression → "talk with your child's doctor" signal** — never a label. This also backstops B4 (a real regression must never be explained away as a "leap").
2. **Communication referral cues (SLP).** Surface, in a *"worth raising with your pediatrician or an SLP"* register: not babbling and not using gestures (point/wave/show) by ~12m; no single words by ~16m; no two-word combinations by ~24m (ASHA *Late Language Emergence* — the Rescorla/Paul <50-words / no-two-word-combos-by-24m criterion); any loss of speech/babble/social skills. **Hearing:** because hearing is the foundation of early talking, any speech/language concern in a baby should also point to a **hearing check** (ASHA) — never an Arbor verdict.

**Status — split:**
- **Substantiated (ships):** the 2–24m surveillance set, ASHA rows, preterm correction (all confirmed sound), the past-band "worth mentioning to your provider" prompts, the ASHA communication referral cues + hearing-check pointer, **and the new regression → act-early prompt** — all surveillance, all route to a provider, none diagnose.
- **GATED / recommend-veto if introduced:** any string that **scores a baby against peers** ("behind," "delayed," "below average," "X% of milestones met" framed as a deficit); any **condition name** surfaced by the engine (autism/ADHD/CP/hearing loss/global delay/speech delay-as-verdict); any **probability/risk percentage**. `monitoring.ts`'s own header already forbids these — hold the line. Keep the `DevScore` "not a diagnosis" note (`devscore.note`) mandatory and never let the ring read as a normed score.

**cited_basis:** CDC "Learn the Signs. Act Early." referral wording + all-ages milestone pages; Zubler et al., *Pediatrics* 2022 (75th-percentile design); ASHA *Late Language Emergence* practice portal + *Communication Milestones*; ASHA early-hearing guidance.
**Honesty flag:** the milestone-age cutoffs are CDC/ASHA conventions, not validated individual cutoffs — keep every prompt "worth raising," never a determination. **HE-translation-sensitive:** "delay/delayed" (עיכוב התפתחותי is a near-diagnosis in HE — keep out of child-specific copy; only "כדאי לבדוק עם רופא הילדים"), "act early" (a calm "כדאי להתייעץ," not an alarm), "regression/loss of skills" (אובדן מיומנויות, never נסיגה התפתחותית as a verdict), "speech-language pathologist" (קלינאי תקשורת — a referral target, not what Arbor is). ## 2. Cross-cutting notes for the Council + Orchestrator

- **Build/sequence dependency.** The plan's sequence is **B0 → B1 → B3 → B2 (gated)** + B4 later. B0/B1 are **shipped and clinically ratified.** **Surveillance refinement** is the cheapest next win (data + register; it carries the one new requirement — regression detection — which is itself a `safe`, provider-routing addition). **B4** ships its *substantiated* normalization half immediately while the **leap-calendar half stays HELD** (recommend-veto). **B3** ships neutral logging + own-pattern reflection while **norm/inference stays HELD**. **B2 is the most gated** — it cannot be build-ready until the EU-MDR read clears the reference-overlay/interpretation surface; the percentile-as-result / growth-verdict / centile-crossing strings are HELD.
- **What this packet does NOT clear.** It substantiates *facts, mechanism, and copy register*. It does **not** clear the **procedural** gates: **child-data capture/egress** for B3's new collections (consent / COPPA-2026 / GDPR, parent = named data controller), the **EU-MDR read** for B2's reference/interpretation surface and B3's inference surface, and `arbor-safety` sign-off on any escalation/safety wiring — those remain Guy/`arbor-safety` decisions, unchanged. This packet lifts only the **clinical soundness/claims** block for the substantiated items.
- **The on-screen strings are the gate.** Every flip to `soundness:pass` is **conditional on the diff matching these specs** — citations are the floor; the displayed copy must match them with zero banned strings. Any diff that ships a percentile-as-result, a growth/feeding/sleep verdict, a leap-week calendar, a "your baby should…" prescription, or a parent-blame narrative reverts to HELD.
