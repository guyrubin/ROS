## 1. Executive takeaway

Badhuiskade 217 is the best-fit **personal-living** asset on the current radar — A+++ BOLD tower, Overhoeks, parking claimed, family/lifestyle/international fit — priced ~€36k under the stated MVA value (€900k vs €944k). It is **not a BRRRR/value-add deal**: no sqm, unit, layout, or rent-uplift upside; the return is area appreciation + energy + liquidity. As a personal residence the economics work; as a buy-to-let it is thin (gross ~4.4%, net likely sub-3.3% after a real VvE charge and the 10.4% investor transfer tax). **Verdict: Proceed-if-conditions-met** — five binary DD gates must clear before any offer.

## 4. Risks / red flags

- **VvE service charge unknown** — note shows €0, implausible for a tower with gym/sauna/cinema/concierge. A realistic €250–€450/mo materially cuts net yield and adds carrying cost. **Highest-impact open item.**
- **Erfpacht (leasehold) status unknown** — Amsterdam is heavily erfpacht. Annual canon vs perpetual buyout swings total cost and financing by tens of thousands. **On the critical path.**
- **Parking right unconfirmed** — included vs separate deed/use-right is a price and resale-value question.
- **MJOP / reserve fund** — a 2021 building should be healthy, but confirm no early-life capex or façade/installation reserve shortfall.
- **VvE restrictions** — rental, short-stay, home-office, and alteration rules; relevant if Guy ever lets it or runs the holding from it.
- **No development upside** — high-rise, finished unit. Downside protection is good (energy, parking, build quality, liquidity); upside is purely market-timing. Do not underwrite this as value-add.
- **Permit:** not on the critical path. Owner-occupier living needs **no permit**; any internal alteration in a 2021 unit is minor/VvE-consent-led, not omgevingsvergunning-led. **Permit dependency: none for the base case.**
