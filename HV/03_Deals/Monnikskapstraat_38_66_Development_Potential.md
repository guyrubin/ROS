- Key DD: confirm current BAG/legal dwelling count, omgevingsplan use, split deed/cadastral requirements, fire separation, utility metering, parking rights, erfpacht conditions, lender stance.

## Risks / red flags

- **Permit and legality:** Dual occupation in marketing copy is not the same as legal split, independent dwelling rights, or bankable rental permissions.
- **Omgevingsplan / mixed destination:** Need exact permitted use, maximum build envelope, and any private-law or erfpacht constraints.
- **Erfpacht:** Both have annual canon; no. 66 re-indexes after 2028, which could affect underwriting.
- **WWS / rental regulation:** High-quality A+ homes may still need WWS scoring; do not assume liberalized rents without WWS advisor check.
- **Execution:** Top-up requires structural capacity, BBL, fire, access/escape, roof details, neighbors, and likely permit.
- **Liquidity:** Large bespoke live/work homes have narrower buyer pool; split legality improves exit.

## Recommendation

### Monnikskapstraat 66 — **Proceed if DD confirms split/top-up path**

This is the better HollandVest-style candidate: lower ask €/m², more existing area, two-address setup, explicit split possibility, parking, and stated top-up angle. It is not cheap, but the optionality is real enough to justify DD.

### Monnikskapstraat 38 — **Hold / backup**

Good house and good live-work flexibility, but ask €/m² is materially higher and new-volume upside is weaker. Only becomes attractive if legal split and/or 28 m² conversion is straightforward, or if price can be negotiated.

## DD checklist

1. Request verkoopbrochure, floorplans, NEN2580 meetrapport, cadastral extract, erfpacht docs, and any prior permits/drawings.
2. Ask broker explicitly:
   - Are there already two legal addresses/dwellings in BAG?
   - Is split already legally possible or only physically possible?
   - For no. 66, what basis supports “can be topped up”?
   - Any pre-consultation with gemeente/architect/constructeur?
3. Pull omgevingsplan/build envelope for both addresses.
4. Ask architect/constructeur for quick massing and structural feasibility: top-up, fire escape, daylight, roof load, stair/access.
5. Ask WWS advisor for split-unit rent potential and liberalization risk.
6. Underwrite separate personal-living case vs investor case because transfer tax materially changes the entry basis.
