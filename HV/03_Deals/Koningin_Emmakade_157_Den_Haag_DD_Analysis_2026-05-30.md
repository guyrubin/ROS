# Koningin Emmakade 157, Den Haag — bid/DD analysis

## Executive takeaway

HollandVest submitted a final bid of **€1,100,000 k.k.** for Koningin Emmakade 157 with **no financing condition** and a **three-week buyer-controlled due-diligence condition**. The asset is a large 1893 corner house in Duinoord, listed on Funda as **332.8 m² NEN2580**, **8 bedrooms**, **2 bathrooms**, **4 toilets**, **own ground**, **energy label F**, protected cityscape context, and asking price **€1,295,000 k.k.**

The bid is strategically defensible because the entry basis is low for this scale/location: **€3,305/m² on Funda area** or **€3,438/m² on BAG registered VBO area**. The main thesis is not as-is family-home ownership; it is **legal/physical apartmentisation**, likely from an already partly-registered two-unit situation toward **3 units** and possibly **4 units**.

The key insight from BAG: the address appears to have **two existing residential VBOs** on the same parcel:

- `Koningin Emmakade 157`: **221 m²**, woonfunctie
- `Koningin Emmakade 157A`: **99 m²**, woonfunctie
- total BAG registered VBO area: **320 m²**

This matters materially: the DD question is not only “can we split a single house into three?” but rather “can we formalise/retain two existing residential objects and create one or two additional independent apartments, and can those be sold or financed separately?”

Recommendation: **Proceed if conditions are accepted**, but make the three-week DD period real and non-negotiable. The bid should only become hard after confirmation of: legal split status, VvE/private-law feasibility, municipal woningvorming/splitting permit pathway, parking/leefbaarheid criteria, protected-cityscape restrictions, structural/fire/daylight feasibility, and capex.

## Strategic thesis

### Why the asset is interesting

1. **Large scale in prime-ish family/international-school location**
   - 332.8 m² in Duinoord is rare and creates optionality.
   - International schools and Scheveningen/centrum access support rental and sale demand.

2. **Low entry basis**
   - €3.3k/m² on listing area is not a retail-renovated-apartment price.
   - The bid is 15.1% below ask.

3. **Existing multi-object signal**
   - BAG already shows two woonfunctie objects: 157 and 157A.
   - This may reduce conceptual resistance to multi-unit use, but it does not prove that further legal splitting/sale is allowed.

4. **Physical apartmentisation clues**
   - Funda notes 8 bedrooms, 4 toilets, 2 bathrooms, kitchenette on first floor, multiple floors, and large volume.
   - Corner position may improve light and layout quality.

5. **Architect sees path to 3 and maybe 4**
   - Ylja’s first read is positive on 3 based on leefbarometer and parking pressure.
   - 4 units may be possible, but should be treated as upside, not base case.

### Why it is risky

1. **Rijksbeschermd stadsgezicht**
   - Facade, roofline, dormers, exterior changes, window alterations, terraces, and visible interventions may be restricted.
   - It may not block internal subdivision, but it can constrain the cheapest route to compliant units.

2. **Energy label F**
   - Upgrading to a financeable/sellable/lendable apartment product likely requires serious insulation/MEP work.
   - In a protected cityscape, exterior insulation/window replacements may be constrained.

3. **Old-building clauses**
   - Age, asbestos, and lead clauses are explicit.
   - Expect hidden capex risk: structural floors, fire compartments, risers, roof, sewer, damp, foundation, asbestos/lead remediation.

4. **Unit formation is not just a design question**
   - Need municipal permit / woningvorming / possibly splitting permit.
   - Need private-law ability to divide/sell: deed, apartment rights, VvE, notarial split, lender requirements.

5. **Parking pressure close to threshold**
   - Architect noted 80–90%. If formal threshold is near 90%, a later measurement/methodology issue can make or break the plan.

6. **Already two VBOs creates opportunity and complexity**
   - Opportunity: existing 157A supports multi-residential fact pattern.
   - Complexity: need understand legal history. Is 157A a separate apartment right, just BAG registration, or internal subdivision without saleable apartment right?

## Base-case, upside-case, downside-case

### Base case — 3 apartments

Assume three legal independent apartments from 320–333 m² total.

- Likely average size: 107–111 m²
- Product: premium family/couple apartments, likely liberalised if WWS supports
- Thesis: robust; aligns with current bid email and architect’s first answer
- Critical path: confirm municipality + legal split + technical/fire feasibility

Base case is acceptable if total development basis remains around **≤€5,250–5,500/m²** and exit/refi can support **€6,250+/m²** equivalent or strong rental income.

### Upside case — 4 apartments

Assume four legal independent units averaging 80–83 m².

- Better liquidity and unit affordability
- More rentable/sellable ticket sizes
- Better acquisition basis per unit
- But higher permit scrutiny, more kitchens/bathrooms/meters/risers/fire doors/acoustic separation, and possibly stronger parking/leefbaarheid objection

Treat 4 units as upside until confirmed by permit specialist and architect.

### Downside case — no further split

If only existing two VBOs can be maintained, or if formal legal splitting fails:

- You own a large, inefficient, old high-capex house/two-unit property.
- Rental yield may still work only if luxury rental demand supports high rents and capex is limited.
- Exit as one luxury family house may be slower and less aligned to HollandVest’s BRRRR/value-add thesis.

In this scenario, the €1.1m bid is not catastrophic because entry basis is low, but the opportunity becomes a lower-return renovation/speculation rather than a high-conviction unit-add deal.

## Three-week DD plan

### Day 1–3: legal facts

1. Request all documents from broker immediately:
   - title deed
   - cadastral extract
   - existing floor plans / NEN report
   - seller questionnaire
   - asbestos/lead info
   - building/renovation permits history
   - energy label report
   - any existing split deed / VvE documents / apartment rights info
2. Ask notary to confirm:
   - Is 157A legally separate beyond BAG registration?
   - Are there existing apartment rights?
   - What is needed to create 3 or 4 saleable apartment rights?
3. Confirm whether buyer can assign to SPV/BV and whether any seller conditions block development intent.

### Day 3–7: public-law feasibility

1. Permit specialist checks current Omgevingsplan / former bestemmingsplan rules.
2. Confirm exact municipal route:
   - omgevingsvergunning for woningvorming?
   - bouwactiviteit / internal alteration?
   - monument/protected cityscape advice?
   - house-splitting or apartment-right split permit?
3. Verify parking pressure and leefbarometer with the exact official methodology and current measurement area.
4. Confirm whether 3 and 4 units are both permitted or whether 4 triggers extra constraints.

### Day 5–12: technical feasibility

1. Architect makes two layouts:
   - 3-unit compliant base case
   - 4-unit upside case
2. Contractor/technical inspector checks:
   - structure and floor loads
   - fire compartmentation and escape routes
   - acoustic separation
   - risers/meters/utilities
   - roof/façade/window constraints
   - damp/foundation/basement
   - asbestos/lead remediation
3. WWS/rental advisor checks whether each proposed unit is liberalised under expected post-renovation condition.

### Day 12–18: financial underwriting

1. Produce capex estimate by scenario:
   - light split
   - full renovation to sell
   - energy upgrade route
   - contingency for old-building/protected-cityscape constraints
2. Get broker BPO for:
   - renovated 3-unit sell-out
   - renovated 4-unit sell-out
   - as-is fallback sale
   - rental values per unit
3. Run financing/refi case with lender assumptions for multi-unit residential.

### Day 18–21: go/no-go

Only go hard if all are true:

- 3-unit legal path confirmed in writing or clearly high probability.
- Existing 157/157A legal status understood.
- Parking/leefbaarheid not a blocker.
- Protected cityscape constraints do not destroy capex/timeline.
- Capex remains within margin envelope.
- At least one clean fallback exists: 2-unit hold/refi or family-house exit.

## Recommendation

**Proceed if conditions are accepted.**

The €1.1m bid is well framed: low enough to compensate for risk, and the DD wording is appropriately broad. The asset has real value-add potential because the purchase basis is low, the area is attractive, the building is large, and BAG already indicates two residential objects.

Do **not** let “architect thinks it can split” become the investment thesis. The investment thesis must be proven as:

1. legal/municipal permission,
2. saleable/refinanceable apartment rights,
3. technically compliant layouts,
4. capex within the margin envelope,
5. credible exit/rent values.

If 3 units is confirmed and capex is not extreme, this is a serious candidate. If 4 units is confirmed, the deal becomes meaningfully more attractive. If the project falls back to only 2 units or one luxury house, renegotiate or walk unless price improves materially.
