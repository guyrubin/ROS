## Priority conclusion

The current DD order should be:

1. **Nieuwendammerkade 24 A** — best risk/reward if opbouw path is real.
2. **Nieuwendammerkade 26 G** — cleanest because permit is stated as already granted.
3. **Bloemendalergouw 43 A** — cleanest ground-up/newbuild lane.
4. **Dorpsstraat Holysloot 52** — promising rural redevelopment, but entitlement-heavy.
5. **Nieuwendammerdijk 536 / Waterglasfabriek** — biggest long-option value, but commercial/planning risk.

The best first phone call is **Nieuwendammerkade 24 A** because the ask is low enough that a 35–40 m² opbouw can create real value if VvE + permit path is confirmed.

Assumptions:

- +35 m² at €5,000/m² capex: total basis €925,000; ~€6,560/m² post-build
- +40 m² at €3,500/m² capex: total basis €890,000; ~€6,096/m² post-build
- +40 m² at €5,000/m² capex: total basis €950,000; ~€6,507/m² post-build

Gross paper uplift before taxes/fees/finance/delay:
- At 141 m² × €8,000/m² less ask and low-cost capex: ~€255k
- At 141 m² × €9,500/m² less ask and low-cost capex: ~€467k
- At 146 m² × €8,000/m² less ask and low-cost capex: ~€278k
- At 146 m² × €9,500/m² less ask and low-cost capex: ~€497k

### Key DD questions

Ask broker:
1. Can you send the **VvE minutes or written VvE confirmation** showing positive position toward an opbouw?
2. Which neighbor received the municipal permit, and can you share the **permit number / drawings / decision letter**?
3. Has an architect confirmed that the same massing is feasible for no. 24 A?
4. Is the proposed +35–40 m² expected to be classified as **GBO wonen**, **business space**, or mixed?
5. Does the 50/50 woon-werk requirement remain after opbouw, and how is it calculated?
6. Are there structural drawings for the existing building, roof load capacity, or a prior engineer opinion?
7. What changes are needed to the split deed / VvE regulations / private parking rights?
8. Are there fire safety, BBL, escape route, or acoustic requirements triggered by the opbouw?
9. Are there any known objections from neighbors or the VvE?
10. What is the seller’s reason for not applying for the permit themselves?

### Go / no-go trigger

Proceed only if:
- VvE support is evidenced in writing;
- neighbor permit is real and comparable;
- architect says permit path is copyable;
- added m² counts as valuable GBO, preferably residential/live-work usable.

- +105 m² at €4,500/m²: total basis €1,267,500; ~€5,761/m² post-build

### Key DD questions

Ask broker:
1. What exact evidence supports the statement that there are “reële kansen” for redevelopment/change of use?
2. Has there been a municipal pre-consultation, principle request, or written planning opinion?
3. What is the current agrarian bestemming and permitted residential use today?
4. Can the te slopen stal volume be reused/replaced as residential volume?
5. Is full replacement newbuild possible or only renovation/extension?
6. Are there protected village/landscape constraints in Holysloot?
7. Soil/asbestos reports for the former agrarian use and barn?
8. Utilities/sewer/water and access constraints?
9. Are neighboring precedents available?
10. Is sale subject to any agricultural, anti-speculation, or municipality conditions?

### Go / no-go trigger

Only proceed after an independent planning consultant validates the residential redevelopment thesis.
