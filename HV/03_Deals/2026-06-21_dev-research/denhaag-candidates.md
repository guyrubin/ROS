# Den Haag — Prime Value-Add Development Candidates

## Takeaway (read this first)

The Den Haag prime herenhuis / heel-pand value-add segment is **real but extremely thin and fast-moving**: average days-on-market in the target districts is ~46 days, asking prices are routinely beaten by >10%, and **almost every "absolute prime + needs full renovation" pand we found sold within the last 3–6 months** (Stevinstraat 79 at €795k, Prins Mauritslaan 33 monument-G at €1.45M, Riouwstraat 19 Archipel-F at €1.6M, Van Beuningenstraat 26, 1e Sweelinckstraat 31). Those are the **proof that the thesis works in Den Haag** — they are textbook NL equivalents of the Antwerp reference — but they are gone.

**Currently buyable (live) that fit the buy-box:** a small set, led by **1e Sweelinckstraat 6** (Duinoord whole herenhuis, reconfig/upside, but €1.65M = above cap) and a few Duinoord/Zeeheldenkwartier herenhuizen. **No live, in-budget, vacant whole-pand with a clean splitsing/optopping permit path was confirmable today** — partly because **Den Haag woningsplitsing has been restricted and only re-opens from 1 April 2026** (material regulatory flag, see Constraints).

**Recommendation for HV:** set up **broker mandates / off-market alerts** with the active prime-Den-Haag brokers (Estata, Frisia, Beeuwkes, Verra, DOEN, SCOOP, Stokman Van Duren) rather than relying on portal scraping — the good ones transact before/at portal listing. Treat the live candidates below as **watch + move-fast**, and use the sold comps to calibrate underwriting.

> **Source-access note:** funda.nl detail pages are anti-bot walled ("Je bent bijna op de pagina"); pararius/huispedia/beleggingspanden listing pages 403 to the fetcher. All facts below are triangulated from **Google snippets of funda/huispedia/broker pages + broker sites that render (Propylo, Estata, klusvastgoed, wonenindenhaag, Diva)**. Each candidate is marked VERIFIED (2 sources) or SINGLE-SOURCE.

Likely **not currently for sale / not the 7-apt play anymore** — confirm directly with Young REI / the listing broker before pursuing. Ticket also likely > €1.5M for the whole. Flag: rijksmonument.
- **Oranjestraat 6-8**, Zeeheldenkwartier (2 monumental herenhuizen → 15 luxury apartments, €250k–€1.195M per unit) — this is a **completed/ongoing developer project selling finished units**, not a raw value-add acquisition. Context only.

## Constraints & red flags HV must underwrite

1. **Woningsplitsing is gated.** Den Haag restricted splitting; expanded/eased rules expected Q3–Q4 2025 and **splitting "possible again from 1 April 2026."** Any split thesis: min **35 m²/unit**, neighbourhood must score good/excellent on Leefbaarometer (Statenkwartier/Archipel/Duinoord qualify), **parking pressure must be <90%** for a permit and then **max 1 of 2 units gets a parking permit.** Confirm current 2026 verordening status before underwriting a split.
2. **Protected cityscape:** **Statenkwartier and Archipel are beschermd stadsgezicht; Duinoord is rijksbeschermd stadsgezicht.** Façade/volume changes (optopping/dakopbouw/uitbouw) face stricter welstand — verify per-pand. Monument status (e.g. Prins Mauritslaan 33 = G monument; Sweelinckplein 74 = monumental 1895) adds restoration cost + permit drag.
3. **Energy label = the value-add tell.** The strongest deals carried **E/F/G labels** (Riouwstraat F, Prins Mauritslaan G) → that's where the €/m² reno uplift lives. Target older + poor-label stock.
4. **Erfpacht:** not confirmed either way on individual panden here — check kadaster per deal.
5. **Speed:** ~46-day DOM and >asking sales mean a portal-only search misses the best stock. Broker mandates are the move.
