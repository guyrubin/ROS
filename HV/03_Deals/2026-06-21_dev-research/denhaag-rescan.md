# Den Haag dev-potential rescan — live sourcing sprint

**Verdict up front:** Of currently-live Den Haag stock in band, **no candidate fully beats the Antwerp Zuidpark reference profile** — and the structural reason is the one the brief predicted: Den Haag's biggest €/m²-discount lever (woningsplitsing) is **gated to 1 Apr 2026 and conditional even after** (min 35 m²/unit + Leefbaarometer 2-yr score + parking <90%), so the cleanest "buy whole, split into 4–5, ~33% discount" play that made Zuidpark a 70-something score is not available here right now. The best DH deals are **deep-reno uplift** and **tenanted-income / uitpond** plays whose value-add does NOT depend on the frozen lever. Two are genuinely worth underwriting; one ties the reference on margin-of-safety but loses on permit-optionality.

**Adversarial verification note:** every price + size below was cross-checked against a second source this run. The single best-fit reno profile I found — **Van Merlenstraat 120** (283 m², 1899, label D, "good foundation, becomes a family home after renovation," Regentessekwartier) — **is now VERKOCHT on Funda** and is therefore excluded from the ranking, not listed as live. Same for 2e Sweelinckstraat 138, Willemstraat 11, Stevinstraat 223 (all sold).

## Sub-score detail (DEV-MODEL §1 weights)

**#1 Van Marumstraat block — 62/100**
- Entry €/m² discount to district (30): **18/30** — ~€4,472/m² vs Valkenboskwartier district ~€4,500–4,800/m² is only a small gap, BUT it's priced on a *let-state / income* basis, so the uitpond-to-vacant arbitrage (vacant DH units sell well above let value) is the real discount. Score reflects latent uitpond equity, not headline €/m².
- Volume/unit-add headroom (20): **14/20** — already 10 units; lever is *consolidate/uitpond/upgrade non-zelfstandig rooms to zelfstandig*, not net-new units. Independent of the split freeze.
- Control & vacancy (15): **7/15** — whole-building control (good) but **fully tenanted** (the cost: no vacant-day-1, exit is staged uitponding as tenants leave).
- EPC/label upside (15): **9/15** — pre-1940, likely poor labels across units = real uplift lever on refi/sale; unconfirmed per unit.
- Permit openness (10): **8/10** — thesis needs **no** splitsingsvergunning (units already exist); strongest on this axis precisely because it dodges the frozen lever.
- Location durability (10): **6/10** — Valkenboskwartier is liquid, student/young-prof demand, but a notch below true-prime Statenkwartier/Archipel.

**#2 Stevinstraat 173 — 55/100**
- Discount (30): **10/30** — €4,260/m² on plot looks cheap but monument status caps GDV and inflates reno €/m²; real discount is modest.
- Unit-add (20): **11/20** — souterrain already independent → de-facto 2 dwellings without a permit; limited beyond that (monument).
- Control & vacancy (15): **13/15** — whole house, own grond, vacant-able.
- EPC/label (15): **6/15** — monument facade limits the cheap envelope wins (no exterior insulation, restricted glazing).
- Permit openness (10): **6/10** — reno/restore route open; any volume add fights welstand + monument.
- Location durability (10): **9/10** — Belgisch Park / coast-adjacent, very durable.

**#3 Sweelinckplein 61 — 41/100**: finished + monument + above-district price = no margin of safety. Discount 3/30, unit-add 6/20, control 12/15, label 4/15, permit 6/10, location 10/10. Listed here only as the band's upper edge and to show what "no lever" looks like.

## Escalation to hv-orchestrator

**Recommend underwriting #1 (Van Marumstraat block)** — it is the only live DH deal that reproduces the reference's whole-building + multi-unit shape with a value-add lever (uitpond + label) that survives the splitsing freeze. The IC question for `hv-underwriting`: does the **vacant-unit uitpond GDV minus staged-vacancy carrying cost** beat the 6.54% BAR hold, and what entry discount to the €1.1M ask makes the uitpond exit clear ≥20% margin-on-cost? Verify the WOZ-per-address and the WWS points on the 9 non-zelfstandig rooms (the €1,980/mo zelfstandige unit at 190 WWS pts is fine; the rooms are the swing factor).

**Watch item for the buy-box:** if Den Haag splitsing reopens cleanly on 1 Apr 2026 (and the target neighbourhood clears the Leefbaarometer + parking tests), the whole-building-to-split thesis becomes scoreable again — at which point raw herenhuizen like the now-sold Van Merlenstraat 120 would re-enter as 65+ candidates. Flag to re-run this scan early April.
