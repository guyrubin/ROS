# BE / Flanders Market Module — Antwerp Development Data Pack (2026)

**For:** HollandVest (NEW to the Belgian market) — underwriting & permitting prime-location development / value-add deals in Antwerp, Flanders.
**Compiled:** 2026-06-21 by research-agent (KK-owned). Hand back to HV lead for the deal decision.
**Posture:** Evidence only. Every number sourced; unverified items flagged. Figures are ranges — confirm address-specific facts with a local notary + architect before committing.

> **Three findings that change HV's model vs. an outdated mental model:**
> 1. **HV pays 12% registration duty, not 2%.** The 2% rate is the buyer's *sole own home* only — it does **not** apply to investment/development purchases. The old **1% IER (deep-energy-renovation) rate was abolished from 1 Jan 2025** and is gone in 2026.
> 2. **The renovatieverplichting was relaxed:** residential E/F properties must reach **EPC label D within 6 years** of the deed (was being communicated as 5; the 6-year term applies from 2025/2026 transfers). It tightens to label C from 2028. This is a hard, dated capex obligation on any value-add purchase.
> 3. **6% VAT on demolition-and-rebuild sales is now a *permanent* regime (since 1 Jul 2025)** — but for **sales to end-buyers it is conditioned** (buyer's sole home ≤175 m², or long-term/social rental). Speculative new-build/transformed units sold to investors are **21% VAT**.

## A. Transaction & holding costs (Flanders, 2026)

### Registratierechten (registration duty)
- **Standard rate — 12%.** Applies to building land, second residences, **and all investment / development real estate**. This is HV's rate. [Derick](https://www.derick.be/nieuws/registratiebelasting-in-2026/) · [Hillewaere](https://www.hillewaere-vastgoed.be/nieuws/registratierechten-vlaanderen-2026-overzicht)
- **Sole own home (enige eigen woning) — 2%.** Conditions tightened from 1 Jan 2026; buyer-must-occupy. **Not available to HV** as an investor/developer. [Derick](https://www.derick.be/nieuws/registratiebelasting-in-2026/)
- **Deep-energy-renovation (IER) reduced rate — GONE.** The former +1% rate for buyers committing to IER was **abolished from 1 Jan 2025** and does not exist in 2026, regardless of renovation intent. *(This is the "rates changed recently" item — do not model 1% IER.)* [Derick](https://www.derick.be/nieuws/registratiebelasting-in-2026/) · [notaris.be (historic)](https://www.notaris.be/nieuws/vanaf-1-januari-2022-1-registratiebelasting-voor-wie-ingrijpend-energetisch-renoveert-sloopt-en-gedeeltelijk-herbouwt)
- **Protected monument — 1%.** Exists, but **tied to the sole-own-home condition** + an obligation to invest 6% of purchase price in the monument within 5 years (total effective 7%). **Not usable by a pure investor/developer.** Flag for any Zuid/historic-centre monument target — only relevant if a principal occupies. [B&A advies](https://www.boekhouder.be/zelfstandige/faq/verlaagd-registratierecht-voorwaarden) · [BIV](https://www.biv.be/nieuws/artikels/binnenkort-slechts-1-registratierecht-te-betalen-bij-aankoop-monument)

### Notary fees + cost stack on purchase
- **Ereloon (notary fee) is legally fixed on a degressive scale** — same at every notary. Rule of thumb ~**€2,300 excl. VAT on a €300k property**; the % falls as price rises. [Hillewaere notariskosten](https://www.hillewaere-vastgoed.be/nieuws/notariskosten-bij-aankoop-woning-2026-belgie) · [notaris.be calculator](https://www.notaris.be/rekenmodules/wonen/aankoopkosten-van-een-woning-en/bouwgrond-berekenen)
- **On top:** 21% VAT on the ereloon; akte/dossier costs, deed registration, mortgage inscription, kadaster extracts, stamp duties — **admin total ~€650–€1,250**. If financed, add credit-/mortgage-deed costs. [Wikifin](https://www.wikifin.be/nl/woning-en-hypothecaire-lening/woning-kopen-bouwen-verbouwen/een-woning-kopen/welke-kosten-heb-je) · [Hillewaere](https://www.hillewaere-vastgoed.be/nieuws/notariskosten-bij-aankoop-woning-2026-belgie)
- **Underwriting heuristic:** on a second-hand value-add buy, model duty (12%) + notary stack ≈ **~13–14% of price all-in** on top of the price.

### VAT (21%) on selling newly-built / transformed units
- A building is **"new" for VAT until 31 Dec of the 2nd year after first use/occupation.** Selling a new or substantially-transformed unit within that window is a **VAT sale**. [Dewaele](https://www.dewaele.com/nl/advies/btw-of-registratierechten-bij-verkoop) · [FOD Financiën](https://fin.belgium.be/en/private-individuals/property/buying-selling/buying-vat)
- **A professional developer/promoter MUST sell under VAT** (not registration duty). Standard rate **21%** on construction value. [Dewaele](https://www.dewaele.com/nl/advies/btw-of-registratierechten-bij-verkoop)
- **Land-VAT point:** in a split structure (one seller for land, one for construction), **the land is always sold at 12% registration duty**, the construction at 6%/21% VAT. Model the land separately. [Dewaele](https://www.dewaele.com/nl/advies/btw-of-registratierechten-bij-verkoop)
- **6% demolition-and-rebuild VAT** is now a **permanent regime (since 1 Jul 2025)** — BUT for **sales to end-buyers it is conditioned**: buyer's sole own home with usable area **≤175 m²**, OR sale/let to a social rental office / long-term (15-yr) rental. **Speculative units sold to investors / above 175 m² → 21%.** Do NOT assume 6% on a dev-for-sale model. [Loyens & Loeff](https://www.loyensloeff.com/insights/news--events/news/demolition-and-reconstruction-at-6-vat-the-new-rules/) · [FOD Financiën](https://fin.belgium.be/en/en/private-individuals/property/build-renovate/demolition-reconstruction/regime-1-july-2025) · [Vandelanotte](https://www.vandelanotte.be/en/news/2025-05-06)

### BTW vs registratie / verkooprecht on a development sale
- **Second-hand building → buyer pays registration duty (verkooprecht, 12%).**
- **New / substantially-transformed building sold by a professional → VAT sale (21%, 6% only if conditions met).** The two are mutually exclusive on the building; **the land under a new-build is the exception → 12% duty.** [Dewaele](https://www.dewaele.com/nl/advies/btw-of-registratierechten-bij-verkoop)
- **Developer advantage of the VAT route:** input VAT on construction/renovation is recoverable when selling under VAT — materially changes net build cost vs. a registration-duty resale. Confirm structuring with a BE tax advisor per deal.

### Company (vennootschap) vs private — high level
- **Investment credit via a company** often gets better terms than a classic home loan; **interest, depreciation and costs are tax-deductible**, optimising taxable profit. [hypotheekwereld](https://hypotheekwereld.be/blog/opbrengsteigendom/slim-investeren-in-vastgoed-in-2026-hoe-haal-je-het-meeste-uit-je-hypothecaire-lening/)
- **Bank underwriting differs:** for a private borrower the bank tests max debt-load (~60%) + living surplus; **for a company it often suffices that rent exceeds the credit charge.** [hypotheek.be](https://www.hypotheek.be/nl/blog/investeren-in-vastgoed)
- A dev-for-sale operation is effectively a professional/VAT activity → a vennootschap is the normal vehicle. *(Confirm corporate-tax, exit-tax and VAT-status specifics with a BE accountant — out of research scope.)*

**Section A confidence:** HIGH on rates/structure (multiple independent sources incl. FOD Financiën primary). FLAG: exact notary stack and the 6%-vs-21% call are deal-specific — verify with a notary per address.

## D. Permits & zoning (Flanders / Antwerp)

### Omgevingsvergunning (Vlaanderen) — process & timelines
- **Simplified procedure (no public inquiry): 60 days** decision deadline.
- **Regular procedure (with public inquiry): 105 days** (120 if the environmental permit commission / OVC is involved). Including the admissibility/completeness check, budget **~135–150 days**; can be **extended once by 60 days**.
- **Public inquiry: 30 days** during the regular procedure — anyone can object (relevant risk in dense prime districts).
- Sources: [Provincie Antwerpen — welke procedure](https://www.provincieantwerpen.be/nl/infopunt-omgevingsvergunningen/welke-vergunningsprocedure) · [VLAIO omgevingsvergunning](https://www.vlaio.be/nl/begeleiding-advies/bedrijfslocatie/bouwen-en-uitbreiden/de-omgevingsvergunning) · [Departement Omgeving doorlooptijden](https://indicatoren.omgeving.vlaanderen.be/indicatoren/doorlooptijd-van-omgevingsvergunningen-en-meldingen)
- File via the **Omgevingsloket**; an **architect is obligatory** for structural / dwelling-creating works.

### Zoning — how to check an address
- **Layers:** Gewestplan (regional plan) → RUP (ruimtelijk uitvoeringsplan) / BPA (older bijzonder plan van aanleg) override locally. The permitted use & number of dwellings/parcel depend on these + municipal ordinances.
- **Check tools:** [Geopunt Vlaanderen](https://www.geopunt.be) (parcel/zoning maps), **DSI** (Digitaal uitwisselingsplatform RUP), and **Stad Antwerpen** bestemmingsplannen. Always pull the **stedenbouwkundig uittreksel/inlichtingen** for the exact address before bidding.

### Opdeling van een woning (creating extra dwelling units) — Antwerp city
- **Always permit-required in Flanders.** [Zonr](https://zonr.be/vergunning/woning-opsplitsen)
- **Antwerp Bouwcode (2024) rule:** splitting a house **with outdoor space >15 m²** into multiple units is **in principle NOT allowed UNLESS** after splitting at least **one unit keeps ≥100 m² net floor area + ≥15 m² adjoining outdoor space.** This caps how aggressively HV can sub-divide character houses. [Antwerpen Bouwcode — Opdelen van een woning](https://www.antwerpen.be/docs/Stad/Stadsvernieuwing/Bestemmingsplannen/SVO_11002_233_10007_00003/SVO_11002_233_10007_00003_0036Opdelenvaneenwon_sv.html)
- Allowable unit count per parcel is governed by zoning (RUP/BPA) + the Bouwcode; some zones forbid multi-family. **Verify per address.**
- **2026 change:** the Flemish Government (6 Feb 2026) approved technical amendments to the exemption decree, effective **1 Mar 2026** — re-check the current exemption list before assuming any works are permit-free. [search result, low-detail — VERIFY on Omgevingsloket]

### Protected monument / stads- en dorpsgezicht (relevant to Zuid / historic centre)
- A **beschermd monument** or protected townscape adds an **Onroerend Erfgoed permit/advice layer** on top of the omgevingsvergunning: works to the protected fabric need heritage approval, design freedom is constrained, timelines lengthen, and façade/interior elements may be untouchable.
- **The 1% registration rate for monuments is own-home-only** (Section A) — so for HV it is a **cost/constraint, not a tax break.** Treat any Zuid/historic-centre monument as higher-risk, longer-timeline. Check status on [Geoportaal Onroerend Erfgoed](https://geo.onroerenderfgoed.be) per address. **FLAG: MEDIUM — heritage permit mechanics described from sector knowledge; confirm scope with Onroerend Erfgoed Antwerpen per building.**

### EPC label & renovatieverplichting — the major BE-specific cost driver
- **Residential:** a buyer of a dwelling with **EPC label E or F** must bring it to **≥ label D within 6 years** of the authentic deed, proven with a new EPC. Misses risk **fines €500–€5,000** (VEKA enforces). Path tightens to **label C from 2028**, B from 2035, A from 2040 (long-term roadmap). [Vlaanderen.be — renovation obligation](https://www.vlaanderen.be/en/moving-housing-and-energy/renovation-obligation-for-residential-buildings) · [certipoint](https://certipoint.be/de-renovatieverplichting-in-2026-van-epc-label-f-naar-d-in-5-jaar/) · [Xenadvies (6-yr relaxation)](https://www.xenadvies.be/2026/02/renovatieplicht-versoepeld-wat-betekent.html)
- **Non-residential:** **small non-res → label C within 5 years** of transfer (since 2022). **Large non-res (>500 m²) → EPC NR + minimum 5% renewable share within 5 years** of transfer/let (since 2023). Different MEPS logic from residential. [Vlaanderen.be renovation obligation](https://www.vlaanderen.be/en/moving-housing-and-energy/renovation-obligation-for-residential-buildings)
- **Why it matters to HV:** any prime-Antwerp character/heritage target almost certainly carries an **E/F label**. The label-D obligation is effectively a **dated, mandatory capex** — but it aligns naturally with a value-add full renovation, so for a deep-reno thesis it's an obligation HV is already meeting, not an extra. The risk is on a **light-touch flip**: a cosmetic refresh that leaves the label below D is non-compliant. Model the EPC target into scope from day one.

**Section D confidence:** HIGH on omgevingsvergunning timelines + renovatieverplichting (primary Vlaanderen.be + provincial sources). MEDIUM on Antwerp opdeling specifics (city Bouwcode quoted, but exact application is parcel-specific) and heritage mechanics. FLAG: the 1 Mar 2026 exemption change and any RUP/Bouwcode detail must be confirmed per address.

## E. Financing (BE dev / buy-to-let, 2026)

- **LTV / quotiteit:** NBB guidance (since 2023) = **max ~90% for an owner-occupied first home, ~80% for second residences / investments.** Model **~80% LTV** for HV investment/dev; lower quotiteit → lower rate. [Maes Group](https://www.maesgroup.be/loan-to-value-quotiteit-hypothecaire-lening/) · [kredietgids](https://www.kredietgids.be/loan-to-value-ltv/)
- **Interest range 2026:** commercial/investment financing **~4.2%–7%** depending on profile (LTV, vehicle, covenant strength). Market expected to stabilise in 2026 after 2022–24 turbulence. [credion](https://www.credion.eu/nl/learning-center/vastgoed-financieren-rente-wat-betaal-je-echt-voor-commercieel-vastgoed-in-2025) · live tracker: [Huisprijzen.be barometer](https://huisprijzen.be/barometer/)
- **Bank appetite / underwriting:** company route — bank often satisfied if **rent > credit charge**; private route — tests max debt-load (~60%) + living surplus. **Bullet credits** (interest-only, principal at exit) are used by investors to maximise leverage/return. [hypotheek.be](https://www.hypotheek.be/nl/blog/investeren-in-vastgoed) · [House of Finance bulletkrediet](https://www.houseoffinance.be/kennis/vastgoed-aankopen-met-een-bulletkrediet)
- **Refinance basis after revaluation:** BE banks refinance off a **new appraised value (schatting)** post-works; the uplift from renovation/split lets HV re-leverage to the LTV ceiling on the higher value (the BRRRR mechanic). **FLAG: MEDIUM — the principle is standard, but BE banks are conservative on speculative dev value; confirm appetite + appraisal basis with the specific lender (KBC/BNP Paribas Fortis/Belfius/ING/Argenta brokers) early.**

**Section E confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH on LTV (NBB guidance is firm) and rate band; MEDIUM on refinance appetite (lender-specific). FLAG: get a real term sheet from 2+ BE banks/brokers before underwriting leverage.

## Cross-cutting flags & gaps for HV
1. **Tax structure is deal-defining and out of research scope to finalise.** The 12% duty vs. 21%/6% VAT split, input-VAT recovery on a VAT sale, and private-vs-vennootschap exit all materially move returns — get a BE real-estate **notary + tax advisor** on the first deal. (Level 3+ decision → HV lead, not research.)
2. **Prime €/m² and rent figures are aggregator-level, not a primary index.** Underwrite only off **sold comps** (Immoweb/Realo/Statbel) + live rentals per address.
3. **EPC label-D obligation + heritage constraints** are the two BE-specific items most likely to surprise an NL-anchored model — both push toward a *deep-reno* thesis over a light flip in prime Antwerp.
4. **Stale-data risk:** rates and schemes here changed in 2025 (IER abolished, 6% demo-rebuild made permanent Jul 2025) and 2026 (2% conditions tightened 1 Jan, exemption decree 1 Mar). Re-verify any tax/permit number at deal time — this pack is a 2026-06-21 snapshot.
5. **Not verified here:** exact prime-centre rent €/m² (only province avg confirmed); precise per-unit opdeling cost; add-floor €/m²; lender-specific dev/refi appetite.
