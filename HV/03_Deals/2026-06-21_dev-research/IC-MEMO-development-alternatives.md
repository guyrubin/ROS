## 1. Executive takeaway

**The value-add thesis is sound; the live assets are priced too rich for a *leveraged* exit at ask.** Across a 5-agent panel (underwriting, permit, architecture, investor, mortgage), **every shortlisted deal is negative on develop-to-sell and sub-1.25 DSCR on BRRRR-hold at the asking price.** This is a *pricing* verdict, not a thesis failure — the deals work on a **negotiated / off-market basis**, not on the pro-forma you'd run at the sticker.

**Two decisions fall out, and the panel is unanimous on both:**

1. **Point capital at Antwerp now; Den Haag is a mandate to set, not a deal to buy.** Den Haag's in-budget prime value-add stock has *already sold* (Stevinstraat 79, Prins Mauritslaan 33, Riouwstraat 19 — all closed in the last 3–6 months), and its core value lever — **woningsplitsing — is legally frozen until 1 April 2026.** Antwerp has live, whole-building, in-budget stock with the split lever (opdeling) open today, at entry €/m² ~55% below the one live Den Haag option.

2. **Pursue the reference itself — A1 Zuidpark (€975k) — as deal #1, but only at a renegotiated basis**, with A3 Dries 20 (€1.15M) as the cleaner-execution, more-financeable in-budget backup. We didn't just find alternatives to the references — we found one (**A3**) that is arguably a *better* deal than the Antwerp reference on execution risk.

**The Funda NL reference (#80818643) is unrecoverable** behind Funda's anti-bot wall; its closest NL analogue is the now-sold Stevinstraat 79 — used as the calibration comp.

## 5. Risks / red flags

- **Refinance gap at ask:** at sourced inputs the BRRRR pull-out leaves €430k+ stuck and DSCR <1.0 on most — the deals only clear on a price cut or genuine off-market basis.
- **BE cross-border:** the 12%-duty vs 21%/6%-VAT structure + private-vs-vennootschap exit are **deal-defining and out of research scope** — engage a BE notary + tax advisor *before* LOI (Level 4). Non-resident foreign vennootschap = top-of-band BE lending rate or decline; pull term sheets from ≥2 BE banks first.
- **A1:** Antwerp Bouwcode opdeling rule (≥100 m²+15 m² outdoor on one unit) can knock 4–5 units down to 3 → halves the GDV story; park-front heritage roofline may refuse the optopping.
- **A2:** if the 3rd unit fails current fire/escape/daylight code, regularisation can be conditioned-on-works or refused → you paid 3-unit money for a 2-unit building.
- **A3:** tenanted → appraised *in let-state* (valuation/refi haircut); "7 units" is a marketing ceiling that the Bouwcode + €/m² economics push back to 5–6.
- **D1:** over the €1.5M cap, retail €/m² entry (€5,710), souterrain dig is the pack's riskiest cost line, split lever frozen to Apr-2026, and the WWS cap can drop reconfigured units into regulated middenhuur → refi fails DSCR.
