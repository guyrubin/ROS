## A. Transaction & holding costs (NL, 2026)

### Overdrachtsbelasting (transfer tax) — rates effective 1 Jan 2026
- **2% — primary residence** (buyer occupies as hoofdverblijf, doesn't qualify for starter exemption).
- **0% — startersvrijstelling**: buyer **18–34** (must be < 35 at transfer), self-occupies, hasn't used it before, property value **≤ €555.000** (up from €525.000 in 2025).
- **8% — investor / second home / let residential** (NEW for 2026, **down from 10,4%**). This is the rate that matters for HV buy-to-let / value-add-to-rent.
- **10,4% — commercial / non-residential** (unchanged).
- A 4% rate exists for certain real-estate share transactions.
- *Model note:* a develop-to-**sell** exit doesn't pay OVB twice — but if HV holds to let, the **8%** entry cost is a hard underwriting line.

### Typical kosten koper (buyer) stack — beyond transfer tax
- **Notary** (deed of transfer + mortgage deed): €1.000–2.500.
- **Buying agent** (aankoopmakelaar, optional): ~1–1,5% of price.
- **Valuation** (taxatie): €500–900 (expect higher for development / value-in-let appraisals).
- **Mortgage advice** (advieskosten): €1.500–3.500.
- **Rule of thumb:** kosten koper total ~4–6% of price *including* the 2% owner rate; for an investor at 8% OVB, total acquisition friction is roughly **9,5–12%** of price.

### VAT vs transfer-tax on new-build / transformed units (on sale)
- **New-build (never used) or "essentially new" after transform:** sale carries **21% VAT, NO transfer tax**.
- **Limited transform = "renovation":** stays existing real estate → **transfer tax** (8% if let-resi), no VAT on sale.
- **Samenloopvrijstelling** (concurrence exemption) can prevent paying both VAT *and* OVB on a new/transformed property bought within 2 yrs of first use — conditions apply; changed from 2025 for share deals.
- **The fork is whether works make it "in wezen nieuwbouw"** (significant change to building structure). This is a fact-and-degree fiscal test — **get a ruling/opinion per project.** Materially changes the model (21% VAT on the whole sale value vs 8% on purchase).

### Corporate vs private acquisition (high level)
- **Box 3 (private):** from 2026, **no interest deduction** and higher tax burden on let property held privately — pushes serious buy-to-let into a structure.
- **BV (corporate):** rental income/gains in corporate income tax; interest deductible; more flexible for development/trading and stacking; setup + admin overhead. Standard NL practice for a value-add/dev portfolio is a holding + project BV(s).
- *Flag:* structure choice is tax + liability + financing dependent — confirm with fiscalist before deal #1.

> **Confidence: High** on rates/stack; **Medium** on the new-build/transform VAT qualification (per-deal). Flag: box-3 2026 burden and BV structuring need a fiscalist sign-off.

## B. Prices, rents, comps (Den Haag prime)

### Buy price €/m² (asking, 2026)
- **Den Haag city-wide:** ~**€4.950/m²** (May 2026); avg transaction ~€534.950 (3-mo to Feb 2026); Q1-2026 avg dwelling ~€767k (skewed by houses), avg apartment ~€405k.
- **Statenkwartier:** ~**€5.482/m² asking**; premium pockets (Statenkwartier/Benoordenhout) cited **> €6.800/m²**.
- **Archipel(buurt):** among the highest in the city — treat as **€5.500–7.000/m²** (verify per street; prices "already very high").
- **Zeeheldenkwartier:** prime-fringe of Centrum, character stock — estimate **€4.800–5.800/m²** (FLAG: not separately sourced, triangulate before model).
- **Centrum:** wide range; near-coast/green pockets up to ~€7.000/m², inland lower.
- **Scheveningen:** coast-proximity drives spread; **near-coast ~€6.000–7.000/m²**, wider district lower.
- **New/renovated sale €/m²:** prime renovated/new stock typically clears at a **10–25% premium** to district average (no clean single source for Den Haag new-build €/m² — FLAG; pull Funda nieuwbouw comps per micro-location for the model).

### Free-sector rent €/m²/month
- **Den Haag avg free-sector:** ~**€24,41/m²** (Q1-2025; ~26% above national ~€19,29). Trend: city free-sector rents rising on tight supply into 2026; prime districts (Statenkwartier, coast, Centrum) sit **above** the city average.
- **Plan around €22–30/m²/mo** for prime free-sector, confirm with live Pararius/Funda Verhuur comps per micro-location.

### Price trend (YoY 2025→2026)
- Existing-home prices **+~5,4%** (2026) nationally-tracked; Den Haag Q2-2025 avg transaction **+~7% YoY** (€506k); some house types (tussenwoning) +14,2% YoY. Net: **mid-single to low-double-digit appreciation**, supply-constrained.

> **Confidence: High** for city-wide + trend; **Medium** for district €/m² (asking-price aggregators, not Kadaster transactions); **Low** for Zeeheldenkwartier and new-build €/m². FLAG: replace district numbers with live transaction comps (Kadaster/Funda) at underwriting.

## C. Development / construction costs (NL, 2026)

### Renovation €/m²
- **Light / cosmetic** (paint, floors, finish): **€500–900/m²**.
- **Medium** (kitchen + bath + partial systems): **€900–1.500/m²**.
- **Full gut / casco→turnkey** (walls out, all systems renewed, kitchen+bath+finish): **€1.500–2.500/m²**. A full interior renovation commonly cited at **€1.200–2.200/m²** (€120k–220k for 100 m²).
- **Cost drivers:** pre-2000 stock with old electrical/sewer/**asbestos** adds **+25–40%**. Turnkey single-contractor adds **~10–15%** over self-managed subs (buys speed + single warranty). Kitchen €5k standard → €25k+ design.

### Cost to add m²
- **Dakopbouw / optopping** (extra storey on roof): **€1.100–3.200/m²** finished; casco prefab ~**€1.500/m²**. 20 m² opbouw ≈ €30k–35k; 40 m² prefab €57,5k–85k, bespoke €74k–110k. Double nokverhoging €16k–30k.
- **Uitbouw / aanbouw** (ground-floor extension, costs garden): **~€2.000–3.000/m²** (FLAG: triangulate; varies with span/foundation).
- **Souterrain / kelder** (dig-down): **€3.000–5.000+/m²** — high variance, water table + underpinning risk in NL coastal soil. **LOW confidence — flag for structural quote.**

### Split a pand into N self-contained units
- Per **new** self-contained unit (own kitchen, bathroom, separate **meters**/utilities, **fire separation**, sound): budget **~€40k–90k per unit** on top of base renovation, depending on whether structure/floors/stairs/entrances must be added. Adding a separate entrance + meter cupboard + fire-rated separation + a compliant 2nd kitchen/bath is the cost core. **LOW-MED confidence — no clean single NL benchmark; price per unit with a contractor + check the Den Haag 35 m² minimum (see §D).**

### Typical timeline (small block, permit + build)
- **Permit:** reguliere 8 wk (most cases) or uitgebreide 26 wk (complex/deviation), each +6 wk extension possible; add design + participation + welstand lead time → **realistically 3–9 months to irrevocable permit**.
- **Build:** small block gut + split + add storey → **~6–12 months** construction.
- **End-to-end (acquire → ready-to-let/sell):** plan **~12–24 months**; FLAG monument/beschermd-gezicht reviews extend the front end.

> **Confidence: Medium** (Dutch contractor aggregator ranges, wide by nature). Always replace with **2 fixed-price contractor quotes** per deal. Souterrain + split-per-unit are the **lowest-confidence** lines.

## D. Permits & zoning (NL / Den Haag)

### Omgevingsvergunning under the Omgevingswet (in force 1 Jan 2024)
- **Reguliere (regular) procedure: 8 weeks** decision term (the default/standard), extendable once by **6 weeks**.
- **Uitgebreide (extended) procedure: 26 weeks**, extendable once by **6 weeks** — for complex applications / deviations from the omgevingsplan.
- **Participation:** the Omgevingswet expects the applicant to involve neighbours/stakeholders early (participatie); municipalities can require evidence of it. Build it into the timeline.

### Bestemmingsplan / omgevingsplan — checking use/zoning
- All former bestemmingsplannen are now part of the municipal **omgevingsplan**. Check an address via the national **Omgevingsloket** (omgevingswet.overheid.nl → "Regels op de kaart") and Den Haag's plan viewer (planviewer/ruimtelijke plannen). Confirm permitted **function (wonen vs other)**, building envelope, storeys, and any beschermd-gezicht overlay before pricing add-m² or change-of-use.

### Den Haag woningvorming / splitsing / onttrekking / kamerverhuur — **MAJOR 2026 change**
- **From 1 April 2026, Den Haag LOOSENS the rules** (Huisvestingsverordening + Nota Woningvoorraad 2025): splitting existing homes into 2+ self-contained dwellings becomes possible again in **many more wijken** (heavily restricted since 2019), **provided neighbourhood leefbaarheid is in order**.
- **Vergunning voor woningvorming / bouwkundig splitsen** still required; new units must be **≥ 35 m² woonoppervlak**.
- **Kamerbewoning** (let to **>2 persons**): still needs a vergunning, but now allowed in many more wijken.
- **Onttrekking / samenvoeging** of woonruimte also vergunningplichtig under the verordening.
- *Underwriting implication:* the 1-Apr-2026 loosening is a **tailwind for split/value-add** — but it is leefbaarheid-conditional and wijk-specific. **Verify the post-1-April rules for the exact address/wijk** and the ≥35 m² floor before modelling unit counts.

### Monument / beschermd stadsgezicht — Statenkwartier / Archipel / Zeeheldenkwartier
- **Statenkwartier = rijksbeschermd stadsgezicht** (126,5 ha, designated 25-Jul-1996). Archipel and the older Centrum/Zeeheldenkwartier fabric carry beschermd-gezicht / monument sensitivity too (verify per address).
- Being in a protected gezicht does **not** auto-make a building a monument — but: (a) exterior changes are tested against the protective omgevingsplan rules, (b) **rijks-/gemeentelijke monument** status (check the monument register per address) triggers a **monumentenvergunning** and stricter material/detail control, (c) demolition/structural change is constrained.
- *Implication:* dakopbouw, façade change, window/material swaps in these wijken face **extra review + likely uitgebreide procedure + longer timeline + higher build spec.** Factor cost + months.

### Welstand
- Den Haag applies **welstand** (architectural-quality test) via its welstandsnota; in protected gezichten the bar is higher. Plans go to the welstands-/monumentencommissie. Budget design iterations + lead time.

### WWS / Wet betaalbare huur (since 1 Jul 2024) — develop-to-rent vs sell
- **Self-contained dwellings now regulated up to 186 WWS points.** 2026 thresholds:
  - **Social ceiling:** 143 points = **€932,93/mo**.
  - **Mid-rent / liberalisation ceiling:** 186 points = **€1.228,07/mo**. New contracts from 1-Jan-2026 between **€932,93 and €1.228,07** (144–186 pts) = regulated **middenhuur**.
- **The initial rent set determines liberalised vs regulated** (not later rent).
- *Underwriting implication:* to let **free-sector** (unregulated), the dwelling must **score >186 WWS points** — pushed by size, energy label, WOZ, outdoor space, quality. Below that, rent is capped by the points table. **This is the single biggest develop-to-rent variable in prime Den Haag** (small old units often fall under the cap). For develop-to-**sell**, WWS is irrelevant — strengthens the sell exit on smaller/older stock. **Run a WWS points calc per planned unit.**

> **Confidence: High** on procedures, WWS thresholds, and the existence + direction of the 1-Apr-2026 Den Haag loosening; **Medium** on exact per-wijk eligibility (verify against the published post-April Huisvestingsverordening). FLAG: monument register + welstand outcome are address-specific.

## E. Financing (NL dev / BRRRR, 2026)

### LTV — buy-to-let / development (verhuurhypotheek / vastgoedfinanciering)
- **Max LTV ~70–80%** of value **in let state** (verhuurde staat), some lenders 70–75%, up to ~85% for select profiles/energy labels. Note value-in-let < vacant/market value, so effective leverage on market value is lower.
- **Rate (2026):** from ~**4,6%**, typically **~5–5,5%** depending on lender, fix, risk class, label.
- **DSCR:** rental income ÷ gross housing costs (interest + repayment + building insurance + erfpacht) must be **≥ 1,20**, often **1,25–1,30** (NIBC 1,25 · RNHB 1,25 · ING 1,30 · Rabo 1,30), specialists up to 1,40.

### Refinance / herfinanciering (BRRRR revaluation)
- After works, **re-appraise at the higher value-in-let** and refinance up to the lender's LTV against the new value — the mechanism to pull capital back out post-renovation/split. Feasibility hinges on the **uplift in appraised let-value** clearing the LTV + DSCR at the new (higher) loan. Specialist lenders (NIBC, RNHB) are the usual venue for value-add/portfolio.
- *Box-3 drag:* from 2026 no interest deduction in box 3 — another reason a **BV** structure tends to win for leveraged dev/BTL (see §A).

> **Confidence: High** on DSCR norms + structure direction; **Med-High** on rate range (moves daily — confirm a live quote per deal). FLAG: erfpacht (ground lease) is common in Den Haag and hits both DSCR and value — check canon/afkoop per address.
