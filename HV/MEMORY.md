## Learned market patterns

- **Underwrite the discount, not the pro-forma.** At ask + sourced 2026 inputs, prime value-add deals routinely print negative leveraged margins and sub-1.0 DSCR — that's a *pricing* signal, not a thesis kill. Rank by margin-of-safety (entry €/m² gap to district); buy below ask / off-market.
- **NL vs BE exit asymmetry:** NL has higher sale €/m² + rent + lower OVB (8%) → develop-to-sell or hold both viable; BE has 12% duty + 21% VAT-on-sale + low rent (€10–11/m²/mo) → develop-to-sell usually beats hold, and the basis discount must be larger to compensate the ~13–14% friction.
- **Den Haag woningsplitsing is gated until 1 April 2026** (then min 35 m²/unit, parking-pressure <90%, 1-of-2 units gets a permit). Any DH split thesis is a "be first when the gate opens" play, not a buy-now play. Antwerp **opdeling is open now** (subject to Bouwcode ≥100 m²+15 m² outdoor rule).
- **BE-specific cost driver:** renovatieverplichting forces non-own-home E/F residential to label D within 6 yrs (→C 2028) — pushes deep-reno, not light flip. The 12% duty / 21%-vs-6% VAT / vennootschap structure is deal-defining → BE notary + tax advisor on deal #1.
- **Funda.nl detail pages are Cloudflare-walled** (confirmed again 2026-06-21) — don't build a scraper; use legible portals + Google snippets + broker mandates. The best dev stock transacts off-market before/at listing.
- For Guy's personal-living-plus-upside brief, Amsterdam Noord currently outranks Zuid and Statenkwartier on combined family logistics, car/parking sanity, modern infrastructure, and future upside. Zuid remains #1 for prestige today; Statenkwartier/Belgisch Park remains best for calm coastal family living.
- BOG/development candidates are tracked separately from personal-living candidates until omgevingsplan/zoning, permit path, VvE/MJOP, erfpacht/own land, parking rights, WWS/rental restrictions, and monument/protected-cityscape constraints have been checked.
