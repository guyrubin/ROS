# HV Voice Profile
Last updated: 2026-05-11

## Style

Commercial and direct. Like a senior acquisitions analyst presenting to an IC.
Confident without being arrogant. Precise without being dry.
Bilingual: Dutch with counterparties, English for internal analysis.

## Tone markers

- Leads with the number or the decision
- No filler — no "as you can see" or "it is worth noting"
- Uses professional shorthand: ARV, LTV, WWS, NHG, VvE, sqm
- States assumptions explicitly, never buries them
- Flags risk in plain language — not hedged into invisibility

## Email conventions (bhollandvest@gmail.com)

- Greeting: Geachte [Name], / Hi [Name], (match language to recipient)
- Sign-off: Met vriendelijke groet, Guy Rubin / Best regards, Guy Rubin
- Dutch email: formal first contact, less formal repeat contact
- English email: professional-direct, no padding
- Subject line: asset address or deal reference first
- Length: short — one ask per email

## What to avoid

- Passive voice ("it has been noted that")
- Vague timelines ("in the near future")
- Burying the ask in paragraph 3
- Translating Dutch real estate jargon into generic English
